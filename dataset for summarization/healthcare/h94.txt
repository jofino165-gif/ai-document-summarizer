The patient visited the cardiology department with complaints of progressive shortness of breath during exertion, episodes of dizziness, chest discomfort, reduced exercise tolerance, and two recent episodes of fainting over the past three months. He has a history of hypertension and high cholesterol but no previous heart surgery.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **148/86 mmHg**, heart rate of **72 bpm**, temperature of **98.4°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **97%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **13.3 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **7,200 cells/µL**, platelet count of **286,000 cells/µL**, serum creatinine of **1.0 mg/dL**, blood urea of **19 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **108 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **5.9%**, total cholesterol of **238 mg/dL**, LDL cholesterol of **160 mg/dL**, and liver function tests within normal reference ranges. Cardiac troponin levels were negative.

Electrocardiogram showed **left ventricular hypertrophy**. Transthoracic echocardiography demonstrated **severe calcific aortic stenosis** with an aortic valve area of **0.7 cm²**, peak transvalvular velocity of **4.5 m/s**, mean pressure gradient of **48 mmHg**, and preserved left ventricular ejection fraction (**58%**). Coronary angiography revealed no significant obstructive coronary artery disease.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Severe Symptomatic Calcific Aortic Stenosis**. Exertional breathlessness, syncope, echocardiographic findings, and valve measurements confirm the diagnosis. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to undergo evaluation for **Aortic Valve Replacement (AVR)** or **Transcatheter Aortic Valve Implantation (TAVI)** based on surgical risk assessment, continue appropriate blood pressure management, avoid strenuous physical activity until definitive treatment, and attend regular cardiology follow-up to monitor disease progression and prevent complications.
