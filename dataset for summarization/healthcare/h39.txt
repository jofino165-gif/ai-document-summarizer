Patient Name: Jonathan Reed

The patient visited the infectious disease department with complaints of persistent fever, night sweats, unexplained weight loss of 5 kg over the past two months, swollen lymph nodes in the neck, recurrent oral infections, chronic diarrhea, and generalized fatigue. He reported a history of unprotected sexual contact several months ago but had never undergone HIV testing.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of 118/74 mmHg, heart rate of 92 bpm, temperature of 100.4°F, respiratory rate of 18 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 98%.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **11.6 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **3,900 cells/µL**, platelet count of **168,000 cells/µL**, and C-reactive protein (CRP) of **22 mg/L**. Kidney function tests demonstrated a creatinine level of **0.9 mg/dL** and blood urea of **18 mg/dL**, while liver function tests were within normal reference ranges. Fasting blood glucose was **95 mg/dL** and HbA1c was **5.4%**.

HIV screening by fourth-generation antigen/antibody assay was positive and confirmed by HIV-1 differentiation testing. The CD4+ T-cell count was **180 cells/µL**, and HIV viral load measured **185,000 copies/mL**, indicating significant immune suppression. Chest X-ray showed no evidence of active pulmonary tuberculosis.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) Infection with Advanced Immunosuppression (AIDS stage)**. Positive confirmatory testing, low CD4 count, high viral load, recurrent infections, and constitutional symptoms support the diagnosis. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to begin combination antiretroviral therapy (ART) immediately, receive prophylaxis against opportunistic infections as appropriate, maintain adequate nutrition, practice measures to prevent transmission, undergo routine CD4 and viral load monitoring, and attend regular infectious disease follow-up visits to improve immune function and long-term health outcomes.
