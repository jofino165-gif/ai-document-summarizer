Patient Name: Jessica Hall

Gender: Female
Report Date: 01-11-2026

The patient visited the infectious disease department with complaints of persistent cough producing blood-streaked sputum, fever, night sweats, fatigue, chest pain, and unintended weight loss of 5 kg over the past six weeks. She reported close contact with a co-worker who was recently diagnosed with pulmonary tuberculosis. The patient denied smoking and had no previous history of chronic lung disease.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **118/76 mmHg**, heart rate of **94 bpm**, temperature of **100.6°F**, respiratory rate of **20 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **96%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **11.7 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **8,900 cells/µL**, platelet count of **312,000 cells/µL**, erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) of **70 mm/hr**, C-reactive protein (CRP) of **36 mg/L**, serum creatinine of **0.9 mg/dL**, blood urea of **17 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **96 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **5.4%**, and liver function tests within normal reference ranges.

Chest X-ray demonstrated cavitary lesions in the upper lobe of the left lung. Sputum smear microscopy was positive for **acid-fast bacilli (AFB)**, and **GeneXpert MTB/RIF** testing confirmed **Mycobacterium tuberculosis** with **rifampicin resistance**. Drug susceptibility testing indicated **Multidrug-Resistant Tuberculosis (MDR-TB)**. CT scan of the chest showed cavitary consolidation without pleural effusion.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Multidrug-Resistant Pulmonary Tuberculosis (MDR-TB)**. Positive microbiological testing, imaging findings, and clinical symptoms confirm the diagnosis. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to begin an individualized second-line anti-tuberculosis treatment regimen under specialist supervision, follow strict infection-control measures, maintain adequate nutrition, undergo regular sputum and laboratory monitoring, and complete the full treatment course to improve outcomes and prevent further drug resistance.
