She has a family history of Type 2 diabetes mellitus and reported a sedentary lifestyle with frequent consumption of sugary foods and beverages.

Fundus examination demonstrated mild **non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy**, while nerve conduction studies showed early **diabetic peripheral neuropathy**. Kidney function remained largely preserved despite evidence of early diabetic kidney involvement.

Persistently elevated blood glucose levels, high HbA1c, microalbuminuria, retinal changes, and nerve conduction abnormalities indicate chronic diabetic complications. The patient was advised to begin intensive diabetes management, maintain a carbohydrate-controlled diet, engage in regular physical activity, monitor blood glucose daily, adhere strictly to prescribed medications, undergo annual eye and kidney evaluations, and attend regular endocrinology follow-up visits to reduce the risk of further complications.
