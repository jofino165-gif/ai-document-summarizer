Patient Name: Brian Mitchell
Age: 47
Gender: Male
Report Date: 28-07-2026

The patient visited the pulmonology department with complaints of persistent fever, productive cough with yellowish sputum, chest pain during deep breathing, shortness of breath, and generalized weakness for the past six days. He reported that the symptoms began after recovering from a viral upper respiratory infection. The patient has a history of smoking for 15 years but quit three years ago. He denies any previous diagnosis of chronic lung disease.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of 126/80 mmHg, heart rate of 102 bpm, temperature of 101.9°F, respiratory rate of 24 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 93% on room air.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of 13.8 g/dL, white blood cell count of 15,400 cells/µL with predominant neutrophils, platelet count of 298,000 cells/µL, C-reactive protein (CRP) of 86 mg/L, and erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) of 48 mm/hr. Kidney function tests showed a creatinine level of 0.9 mg/dL and blood urea of 18 mg/dL, both within normal limits. Liver function tests were also within normal reference ranges.

Chest X-ray demonstrated consolidation in the right lower lobe consistent with bacterial pneumonia. Sputum culture identified Streptococcus pneumoniae as the causative organism, and blood cultures were negative. Fasting blood glucose was 97 mg/dL and HbA1c was 5.4%, indicating normal glucose control.

The physician concluded that the patient has Community-Acquired Bacterial Pneumonia affecting the right lower lung. Elevated inflammatory markers, increased white blood cell count, reduced oxygen saturation, and chest X-ray findings support the diagnosis. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain normal. The patient was advised to complete the prescribed course of antibiotics, maintain adequate hydration, get sufficient rest, monitor body temperature and breathing, avoid smoking and exposure to respiratory irritants, and attend follow-up evaluations to ensure complete recovery and prevent complications.
