Patient Name: Jennifer White
Age: 41
Gender: Female
Report Date: 12-09-2026

The patient visited the gastroenterology department with complaints of persistent abdominal pain, frequent loose stools mixed with blood, weight loss of approximately 5 kg over the past four months, fatigue, reduced appetite, and intermittent fever. She also reported painful mouth ulcers and occasional joint pain involving both knees. The patient has no history of smoking but has a brother diagnosed with inflammatory bowel disease.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **122/78 mmHg**, heart rate of **88 bpm**, temperature of **99.1°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **98%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **10.7 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **11,200 cells/µL**, platelet count of **342,000 cells/µL**, erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) of **68 mm/hr**, C-reactive protein (CRP) of **48 mg/L**, serum albumin of **3.1 g/dL**, and fecal calprotectin of **645 µg/g**. Kidney function tests, liver function tests, fasting blood glucose, and HbA1c were within normal reference ranges.

Colonoscopy demonstrated patchy areas of deep ulceration, cobblestone appearance of the intestinal mucosa, and skip lesions involving the terminal ileum and ascending colon. Histopathological examination of biopsy specimens confirmed chronic transmural inflammation consistent with **Crohn's Disease**. Magnetic Resonance Enterography (MRE) showed mild thickening of the terminal ileum without evidence of fistula formation.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Moderate to Severe Crohn's Disease**, an inflammatory bowel disease affecting the terminal ileum and colon. Elevated inflammatory markers, anemia, hypoalbuminemia, increased fecal calprotectin, colonoscopy findings, and biopsy results confirm the diagnosis. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to begin immunosuppressive and biologic therapy as prescribed, maintain adequate nutrition and hydration, avoid foods that aggravate symptoms, undergo regular gastroenterology follow-up visits, and receive periodic imaging and colonoscopic evaluations to monitor disease activity and prevent long-term complications.
