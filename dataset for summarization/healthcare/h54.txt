Patient Name: Grace Nelson
Age: 26
Gender: Female
Report Date: 02-10-2026

The patient visited the infectious disease department with complaints of persistent fever, severe joint pain affecting the wrists, ankles, and knees, muscle pain, headache, skin rash, and extreme fatigue for the past five days. She reported recent travel to an area experiencing a mosquito-borne viral outbreak and multiple mosquito bites before the onset of symptoms. She denied cough, abdominal pain, or urinary symptoms.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **112/72 mmHg**, heart rate of **96 bpm**, temperature of **101.8°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **99%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **13.5 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **4,200 cells/µL**, platelet count of **186,000 cells/µL**, C-reactive protein (CRP) of **26 mg/L**, serum creatinine of **0.8 mg/dL**, blood urea of **16 mg/dL**, ALT (SGPT) of **44 U/L**, and AST (SGOT) of **40 U/L**. Fasting blood glucose and HbA1c were within normal reference ranges.

Serological testing was positive for **Chikungunya virus IgM antibodies** and negative for Dengue NS1 antigen and malaria parasites. Joint ultrasound demonstrated mild synovial inflammation without joint erosion. Chest X-ray showed no abnormalities.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Acute Chikungunya Virus Infection**. Positive serological testing, severe joint pain, fever, rash, and recent mosquito exposure strongly support the diagnosis. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain largely normal. The patient was advised to maintain adequate hydration, get sufficient rest, use prescribed pain-relieving medications, perform gentle joint exercises after the acute illness subsides, avoid mosquito exposure to reduce transmission risk, and attend follow-up evaluations if persistent joint pain or new symptoms develop.
