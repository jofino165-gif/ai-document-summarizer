Patient Name: Kevin Murphy
Age: 56
Gender: Male
Report Date: 27-11-2026

The patient visited the gastroenterology department with complaints of persistent abdominal pain, chronic diarrhea mixed with blood, unintended weight loss of 7 kg, fatigue, and frequent urgency to pass stools over the past six months. He also reported intermittent low-grade fever and joint pain affecting both knees. There was no family history of inflammatory bowel disease.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **122/78 mmHg**, heart rate of **90 bpm**, temperature of **99.1°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **98%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **10.7 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **11,400 cells/µL**, platelet count of **396,000 cells/µL**, erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) of **72 mm/hr**, C-reactive protein (CRP) of **54 mg/L**, serum albumin of **3.0 g/dL**, serum creatinine of **0.9 mg/dL**, blood urea of **18 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **96 mg/dL**, and HbA1c of **5.4%**.

Colonoscopy demonstrated patchy areas of deep ulceration, cobblestone appearance, and skip lesions involving the terminal ileum and ascending colon. Histopathological examination of biopsy specimens revealed **transmural chronic granulomatous inflammation**, confirming **Crohn's Disease**. Stool cultures were negative for bacterial pathogens and parasites.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Moderate-to-Severe Crohn's Disease** with involvement of the terminal ileum and colon. Chronic gastrointestinal symptoms, elevated inflammatory markers, characteristic colonoscopic findings, and biopsy results confirm the diagnosis. Kidney function and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to begin immunosuppressive and biologic therapy as prescribed, maintain adequate nutrition, avoid smoking, undergo regular colonoscopic surveillance, attend routine gastroenterology follow-up visits, and seek immediate medical attention if severe abdominal pain, bowel obstruction, or persistent gastrointestinal bleeding develops.
