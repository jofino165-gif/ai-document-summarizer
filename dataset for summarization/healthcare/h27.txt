Patient Name: Samuel Foster
Age: 57
Gender: Male
Report Date: 09-08-2026

The patient visited the oncology department with complaints of persistent cough lasting more than three months, coughing up small amounts of blood, chest pain, progressive weight loss of 9 kg over the past five months, shortness of breath, and persistent fatigue. He has a smoking history of 35 pack-years and continued smoking until recently. The patient also reported loss of appetite and occasional hoarseness of voice.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of 132/84 mmHg, heart rate of 92 bpm, temperature of 98.6°F, respiratory rate of 22 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 95%.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of 11.6 g/dL, white blood cell count of 9,400 cells/µL, platelet count of 326,000 cells/µL, serum creatinine of 1.0 mg/dL, blood urea of 18 mg/dL, fasting blood glucose of 99 mg/dL, and HbA1c of 5.4%. Liver function tests were within normal reference ranges.

Chest CT scan demonstrated a 4.8 cm irregular mass in the upper lobe of the right lung with enlargement of nearby mediastinal lymph nodes. Bronchoscopy with tissue biopsy confirmed **non-small cell lung carcinoma (NSCLC)**, specifically adenocarcinoma. PET-CT imaging revealed regional lymph node involvement without evidence of distant metastasis.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Stage IIIA Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)**. Persistent cough, hemoptysis, weight loss, imaging findings, and biopsy results are consistent with the diagnosis. Mild anemia is likely related to chronic disease. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to consult a multidisciplinary oncology team to discuss surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and targeted treatment options where appropriate. Smoking cessation, nutritional support, pulmonary rehabilitation, and regular oncology follow-up were strongly recommended to improve treatment outcomes and quality of life.
