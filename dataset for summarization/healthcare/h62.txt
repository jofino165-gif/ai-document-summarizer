Patient Name: Andrew Collins
Age: 59
Gender: Male
Report Date: 18-10-2026

The patient visited the urology department with complaints of frequent urination, difficulty starting urination, weak urinary stream, incomplete bladder emptying, waking multiple times at night to urinate, and occasional blood in the urine over the past five months. He denied fever or burning during urination. His father was diagnosed with prostate cancer at the age of 72.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **132/84 mmHg**, heart rate of **82 bpm**, temperature of **98.4°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **98%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **12.9 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **7,300 cells/µL**, platelet count of **278,000 cells/µL**, serum creatinine of **1.0 mg/dL**, blood urea of **20 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **98 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **5.5%**, and liver function tests within normal reference ranges. Prostate-Specific Antigen (**PSA**) level was **18.4 ng/mL**, significantly above the normal range.

Digital rectal examination identified a hard irregular prostate gland. Multiparametric MRI of the prostate demonstrated a **2.8 cm lesion** with extracapsular extension. Ultrasound-guided prostate biopsy confirmed **Prostatic Adenocarcinoma** with a **Gleason score of 4+4=8**. Bone scan and CT imaging showed no evidence of distant metastasis.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Localized High-Risk Prostate Adenocarcinoma**. Elevated PSA, abnormal digital rectal examination, MRI findings, and biopsy results confirm the diagnosis. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to consult a multidisciplinary urology and oncology team to discuss radical prostatectomy, radiation therapy, and androgen deprivation therapy as appropriate. Regular PSA monitoring, follow-up imaging, and long-term surveillance were recommended to assess treatment response and detect disease recurrence.
