The patient visited the cardiology department with complaints of severe crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, profuse sweating, nausea, shortness of breath, and extreme weakness that began two hours before arrival at the emergency department. He has a medical history of hypertension, Type 2 diabetes mellitus, hyperlipidemia, and chronic smoking for 40 years.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **92/60 mmHg**, heart rate of **116 bpm**, temperature of **98.5°F**, respiratory rate of **24 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **92%** on room air.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **13.1 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **12,600 cells/µL**, platelet count of **298,000 cells/µL**, fasting blood glucose of **246 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **9.4%**, serum creatinine of **1.3 mg/dL**, blood urea of **28 mg/dL**, total cholesterol of **258 mg/dL**, LDL cholesterol of **182 mg/dL**, HDL cholesterol of **36 mg/dL**, triglycerides of **244 mg/dL**, and markedly elevated **high-sensitivity cardiac troponin-I** levels.

Electrocardiogram (ECG) demonstrated **ST-segment elevation in leads V1–V4**, consistent with an **acute anterior wall ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction (STEMI)**. Emergency coronary angiography revealed **100% occlusion of the proximal left anterior descending (LAD) coronary artery**. Echocardiography showed an **LVEF of 38%** with anterior wall hypokinesia.

The physician concluded that the patient has an **Acute Anterior Wall ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction (STEMI)** caused by complete occlusion of the left anterior descending coronary artery. ECG changes, elevated cardiac biomarkers, coronary angiography, and clinical presentation confirm the diagnosis. Mild kidney impairment is present, likely related to reduced cardiac output. The patient was advised to undergo immediate **primary percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI)** with stent placement, begin dual antiplatelet therapy, high-intensity statin therapy, and other guideline-directed cardiac medications, participate in cardiac rehabilitation after stabilization, stop smoking completely, and maintain strict control of diabetes, blood pressure, and cholesterol to reduce the risk of future cardiovascular events.
