Patient Name: George Mitchell
Age: 69

Report Date: 07-11-2026

The patient visited the neurology department with complaints of resting tremor in the right hand, stiffness of both arms, slowness of movement, difficulty maintaining balance, and shuffling gait that had gradually worsened over the past two years. Family members also noticed reduced facial expression, softer speech, and smaller handwriting. There was no history of stroke or head injury.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **130/82 mmHg**, heart rate of **74 bpm**, temperature of **98.4°F**, respiratory rate of **16 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **98%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **13.7 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **6,800 cells/µL**, platelet count of **276,000 cells/µL**, fasting blood glucose of **98 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **5.5%**, serum creatinine of **0.9 mg/dL**, blood urea of **18 mg/dL**, vitamin B12 level of **480 pg/mL**, and thyroid function tests within normal reference ranges.

MRI of the brain showed mild age-related cerebral changes without evidence of stroke, tumor, or normal pressure hydrocephalus. Neurological examination demonstrated **resting tremor**, **bradykinesia**, **muscle rigidity**, **postural instability**, and reduced arm swing during walking. The patient's symptoms improved following a trial of **levodopa**, supporting the diagnosis.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Parkinson's Disease**. Progressive motor symptoms, neurological examination findings, and a positive response to levodopa strongly support the diagnosis, while imaging excluded other structural neurological disorders. Kidney function, liver function, thyroid function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to begin long-term antiparkinsonian medication as prescribed, participate in regular physiotherapy and balance training, maintain daily physical activity, implement fall-prevention strategies at home, attend routine neurology follow-up visits, and seek medical attention if swallowing difficulties, frequent falls, or worsening mobility develop.
