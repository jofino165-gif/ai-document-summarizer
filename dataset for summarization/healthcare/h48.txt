Patient Name: Lisa Morgan

The patient visited the oncology department with complaints of persistent abdominal bloating, pelvic pain, early satiety, frequent urination, increasing abdominal girth, and unexplained weight loss over the past four months. She reached menopause five years ago and has a family history of ovarian cancer in her older sister. The patient denied any history of gastrointestinal disorders.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **126/80 mmHg**, heart rate of **86 bpm**, temperature of **98.4°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **99%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **11.4 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **7,600 cells/µL**, platelet count of **334,000 cells/µL**, serum creatinine of **0.9 mg/dL**, blood urea of **18 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **96 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **5.5%**, and liver function tests within normal reference ranges.

Serum **CA-125** level was **468 U/mL**, significantly above the normal reference range. Transvaginal ultrasound demonstrated a complex **8.2 cm left ovarian mass** with solid and cystic components. Contrast-enhanced CT scan of the abdomen and pelvis showed enlargement of the left ovary with omental involvement but no evidence of distant organ metastasis. Surgical biopsy confirmed **High-Grade Serous Ovarian Carcinoma**.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Stage III Epithelial Ovarian Cancer** with regional spread to the omentum. Elevated CA-125 levels, imaging findings, and biopsy results confirm the diagnosis. Mild anemia is likely associated with chronic disease. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to consult a gynecologic oncology team for cytoreductive surgery followed by chemotherapy, maintain adequate nutritional support, attend regular oncology follow-up visits, and undergo periodic imaging and CA-125 monitoring to evaluate treatment response and detect disease recurrence.
