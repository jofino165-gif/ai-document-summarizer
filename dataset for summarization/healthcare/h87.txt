The patient visited the hematology department with complaints of persistent fatigue, frequent headaches, dizziness, itching after taking a warm bath, blurred vision, facial redness, and a burning sensation in the hands and feet over the past six months. She denied any history of chronic lung disease, smoking, or living at high altitude.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **150/92 mmHg**, heart rate of **82 bpm**, temperature of **98.5°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **98%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **19.2 g/dL**, hematocrit of **58%**, white blood cell count of **12,400 cells/µL**, platelet count of **498,000 cells/µL**, serum erythropoietin level of **2.8 mIU/mL** (low), serum creatinine of **0.9 mg/dL**, blood urea of **18 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **96 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **5.4%**, and liver function tests within normal reference ranges.

Peripheral blood testing demonstrated a **positive JAK2 V617F mutation**. Bone marrow biopsy revealed **hypercellular marrow with panmyelosis**, consistent with a myeloproliferative neoplasm. Abdominal ultrasound demonstrated mild splenomegaly without focal lesions.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Polycythemia Vera**, a chronic myeloproliferative disorder characterized by excessive red blood cell production. Elevated hemoglobin, hematocrit, low erythropoietin level, positive JAK2 mutation, and bone marrow findings confirm the diagnosis. The patient is at increased risk of blood clots and cardiovascular complications. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to undergo regular therapeutic phlebotomy, begin low-dose aspirin therapy if not contraindicated, consider cytoreductive therapy based on risk assessment, maintain adequate hydration, attend routine hematology follow-up visits, and seek immediate medical attention if symptoms of stroke, heart attack, or thrombosis develop.
