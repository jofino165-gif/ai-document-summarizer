The patient visited the infectious disease department with complaints of high-grade fever, severe headache, neck stiffness, nausea, repeated vomiting, sensitivity to bright light, and increasing confusion for the past two days. Family members reported that the patient became increasingly drowsy on the day of admission. There was no recent history of head injury, but the patient had experienced an untreated upper respiratory tract infection one week earlier.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **108/68 mmHg**, heart rate of **118 bpm**, temperature of **103.4°F**, respiratory rate of **24 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **97%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **13.6 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **18,200 cells/µL** with marked neutrophilia, platelet count of **284,000 cells/µL**, C-reactive protein (CRP) of **126 mg/L**, serum creatinine of **1.0 mg/dL**, blood urea of **20 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **98 mg/dL**, and HbA1c of **5.4%**. Liver function tests were within normal reference ranges.

Lumbar puncture demonstrated cloudy cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) with elevated opening pressure, markedly increased neutrophils, elevated protein concentration, and reduced glucose levels. CSF Gram stain identified **Streptococcus pneumoniae**, and bacterial culture confirmed the organism. CT scan of the brain showed no evidence of intracranial hemorrhage or mass lesion.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Acute Bacterial Meningitis caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae**. Clinical symptoms, CSF analysis, and microbiological findings confirm the diagnosis. The patient is at high risk of serious neurological complications if treatment is delayed. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to begin immediate intravenous antibiotic therapy, receive supportive hospital care, undergo close neurological monitoring, maintain adequate hydration, complete the full course of treatment, and attend follow-up evaluations to assess recovery and detect any long-term neurological complications.
