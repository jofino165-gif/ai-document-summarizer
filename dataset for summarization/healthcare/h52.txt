Patient Name: Steven Brown

The patient visited the hematology department with complaints of persistent fatigue, recurrent fever, frequent infections, easy bruising, bleeding gums, and unintentional weight loss of 7 kg over the past three months. He also reported occasional bone pain involving the hips and sternum. There was no previous history of blood disorders or cancer.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of 122/78 mmHg, heart rate of 92 bpm, temperature of 100.2°F, respiratory rate of 18 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 98%.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **8.9 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **48,600 cells/µL** with **38% blast cells**, platelet count of **42,000 cells/µL**, lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) of **820 U/L**, serum creatinine of **1.0 mg/dL**, blood urea of **20 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **96 mg/dL**, and HbA1c of **5.4%**. Liver function tests were within normal reference ranges.

Peripheral blood smear demonstrated numerous immature blast cells. Bone marrow aspiration and biopsy revealed hypercellular marrow with **more than 30% myeloblasts**. Flow cytometry confirmed the diagnosis of **Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)**. Chest X-ray and abdominal ultrasound showed no evidence of organ involvement outside the bone marrow.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)**. Severe anemia, thrombocytopenia, elevated blast cells, and bone marrow biopsy findings confirm the diagnosis. Kidney function, liver function, and blood glucose levels remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to begin induction chemotherapy immediately under the supervision of a hematology-oncology team, receive supportive blood product transfusions as required, undergo infection prevention measures, attend regular laboratory monitoring, and discuss stem cell transplantation if clinically appropriate.
