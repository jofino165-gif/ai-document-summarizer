The patient visited the endocrinology department with complaints of persistent fatigue, increased thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss of 8 kg over the past three months, blurred vision, and recurrent skin infections. She reported a family history of Type 2 diabetes mellitus and admitted to consuming a high-calorie diet with minimal physical activity.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **142/88 mmHg**, heart rate of **86 bpm**, temperature of **98.5°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **98%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **13.0 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **7,400 cells/µL**, platelet count of **288,000 cells/µL**, fasting blood glucose of **286 mg/dL**, postprandial blood glucose of **382 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **11.3%**, serum creatinine of **1.1 mg/dL**, blood urea of **22 mg/dL**, urine microalbumin of **74 mg/g**, total cholesterol of **248 mg/dL**, LDL cholesterol of **170 mg/dL**, HDL cholesterol of **39 mg/dL**, and triglycerides of **238 mg/dL**.

Fundus examination revealed **moderate non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy**, while urine analysis demonstrated persistent microalbuminuria without significant reduction in kidney function. Peripheral neurological examination showed reduced vibration sensation in both feet, consistent with early diabetic peripheral neuropathy.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Poorly Controlled Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus** with early **diabetic nephropathy**, **diabetic neuropathy**, and **moderate non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy**. Persistently elevated blood glucose, high HbA1c, retinal changes, neuropathy, and microalbuminuria indicate chronic diabetic complications. The patient was advised to begin intensive blood glucose management, follow a diabetic meal plan, engage in regular physical activity, monitor blood glucose daily, adhere strictly to prescribed medications, undergo annual eye and kidney evaluations, and attend regular endocrinology follow-up visits to prevent progression of diabetic complications.
