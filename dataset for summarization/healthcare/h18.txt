The patient visited the endocrinology department with complaints of increased thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss, persistent fatigue, blurred vision, and occasional numbness in both feet over the past three months. She has a family history of Type 2 diabetes mellitus and reports a sedentary lifestyle with frequent consumption of sugary beverages and processed foods. She denies smoking or alcohol consumption.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of 146/92 mmHg, heart rate of 88 bpm, temperature of 98.5°F, respiratory rate of 18 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 98%.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of 13.1 g/dL, white blood cell count of 7,500 cells/µL, platelet count of 284,000 cells/µL, fasting blood glucose of 226 mg/dL, postprandial blood glucose of 312 mg/dL, and HbA1c of 10.1%.

The lipid profile showed total cholesterol of 251 mg/dL, LDL cholesterol of 175 mg/dL, HDL cholesterol of 36 mg/dL, and triglycerides of 241 mg/dL. Kidney function tests demonstrated a creatinine level of 1.2 mg/dL and blood urea of 23 mg/dL. Urinalysis revealed glucosuria and mild microalbuminuria. Liver function tests showed ALT (SGPT) of 48 U/L and AST (SGOT) of 42 U/L, indicating mild elevation.

The physician concluded that the patient has poorly controlled Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus with associated hypertension, dyslipidemia, and early diabetic nephropathy. Elevated HbA1c, persistent hyperglycemia, and the presence of microalbuminuria indicate an increased risk of kidney damage if glycemic control is not improved. Mildly elevated liver enzymes may suggest early non-alcoholic fatty liver disease. The patient was advised to begin intensive diabetes treatment, maintain a strict diabetic diet, increase physical activity, monitor blood glucose and blood pressure regularly, take prescribed medications consistently, undergo periodic kidney function assessments, and attend routine endocrinology follow-up visits to reduce the risk of long-term complications.
