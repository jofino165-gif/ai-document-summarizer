Patient Name: Steven Harris
Age: 49
Gender: Male
Report Date: 17-12-2026

The patient visited the neurology department with complaints of progressive weakness in both hands, muscle twitching, difficulty speaking, frequent muscle cramps, and increasing difficulty swallowing over the past ten months. He also reported weight loss and reduced grip strength. There was no history of stroke, head injury, or sensory loss.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **128/82 mmHg**, heart rate of **80 bpm**, temperature of **98.4°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **98%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **13.4 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **7,100 cells/µL**, platelet count of **280,000 cells/µL**, serum creatinine of **0.9 mg/dL**, blood urea of **18 mg/dL**, fasting blood glucose of **96 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **5.4%**, vitamin B12 level of **465 pg/mL**, thyroid function tests within normal reference ranges, and creatine kinase (CK) of **328 U/L** (mildly elevated).

Nerve conduction studies showed preserved sensory nerve function with reduced motor responses. Electromyography (EMG) demonstrated widespread active and chronic denervation involving the upper and lower limbs. MRI of the brain and cervical spine excluded structural neurological disease.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)**, a progressive motor neuron disease. Progressive muscle weakness, bulbar symptoms, electromyography findings, and preservation of sensory function strongly support the diagnosis. Kidney function, thyroid function, vitamin B12 levels, and blood glucose remain within normal limits. The patient was advised to begin disease-modifying therapy as prescribed, participate in multidisciplinary rehabilitation, undergo speech and swallowing evaluation, maintain adequate nutritional support, attend regular neurology follow-up visits, and discuss advanced care planning as the disease progresses.
