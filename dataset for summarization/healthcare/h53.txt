Patient Name: Mark Phillips

Gender: Male
Report Date: 30-09-2026

The patient visited the vascular surgery department with complaints of severe pain in the left calf while walking short distances, numbness in the left foot, coldness of the lower limb, and a non-healing ulcer over the left great toe for the past three months. He has a medical history of long-standing Type 2 diabetes mellitus, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and a smoking history of 40 years.

Vital signs recorded during examination showed a blood pressure of **158/94 mmHg**, heart rate of **88 bpm**, temperature of **98.4°F**, respiratory rate of **18 breaths per minute**, and oxygen saturation of **97%**.

Laboratory investigations revealed a hemoglobin level of **13.4 g/dL**, white blood cell count of **8,200 cells/µL**, platelet count of **294,000 cells/µL**, fasting blood glucose of **214 mg/dL**, HbA1c of **9.4%**, total cholesterol of **252 mg/dL**, LDL cholesterol of **176 mg/dL**, HDL cholesterol of **39 mg/dL**, triglycerides of **226 mg/dL**, serum creatinine of **1.2 mg/dL**, and blood urea of **24 mg/dL**.

Ankle-Brachial Index (ABI) measured **0.52** in the left leg, indicating severe peripheral arterial disease. Doppler ultrasound and CT angiography demonstrated significant narrowing of the left superficial femoral artery with approximately **85% stenosis** and reduced blood flow to the distal lower limb. No evidence of deep vein thrombosis was identified.

The physician concluded that the patient has **Peripheral Arterial Disease (PAD)** with critical limb ischemia caused by advanced atherosclerosis. Poorly controlled diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia, and long-term smoking have significantly contributed to the disease. The patient was advised to stop smoking immediately, achieve strict blood glucose and blood pressure control, begin antiplatelet and cholesterol-lowering therapy as prescribed, follow a supervised walking rehabilitation program, undergo vascular intervention if recommended, and attend regular vascular surgery follow-up visits to reduce the risk of limb loss.
