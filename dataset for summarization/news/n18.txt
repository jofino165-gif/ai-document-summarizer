Headline:
Global Wheat Prices Rise Following Reduced Harvest in Major Producing Countries

News Content:
Several of the world's largest wheat-producing countries reported lower-than-expected harvests due to prolonged drought and unfavorable weather conditions. As a result, global wheat prices have increased by nearly 12% over the past month. Food manufacturers have warned that the rising cost of wheat may lead to higher prices for bread, flour, pasta, and other wheat-based products. Economists expect the impact to continue until the next harvest season improves supply.
