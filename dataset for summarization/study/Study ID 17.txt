Topic:
Computer Networks

Uploaded Notes:
Computer Networks refer to the interconnection of two or more computers and other digital devices that communicate and share resources through communication channels. Networks enable users to exchange data, access shared hardware and software, and communicate efficiently over short and long distances. Computer networking forms the foundation of modern internet communication and digital services.

A computer network consists of devices such as computers, servers, routers, switches, hubs, access points, and communication media. Data is transmitted through wired media such as Ethernet cables and fiber optics or wireless technologies such as Wi-Fi and Bluetooth. Network communication follows standardized protocols including TCP/IP, HTTP, HTTPS, FTP, SMTP, and DNS to ensure reliable data transmission.

Computer networks are classified into several types based on geographical coverage, including Local Area Network (LAN), Metropolitan Area Network (MAN), Wide Area Network (WAN), Personal Area Network (PAN), and Campus Area Network (CAN). Each type serves different communication requirements.

Networking devices such as routers direct data between networks, switches connect devices within a network, and firewalls protect networks from unauthorized access. Modern networks also support cloud computing, Internet of Things (IoT), video conferencing, online education, and digital banking.

The advantages of computer networks include resource sharing, fast communication, centralized data management, remote access, scalability, and reduced operational costs. Challenges include network security threats, hardware failures, bandwidth limitations, network congestion, and maintenance complexity.

Summary:
Computer Networks connect multiple devices to enable communication, resource sharing, and data exchange using networking hardware and communication protocols. Networks support internet services, cloud computing, online collaboration, and digital communication across homes, businesses, and educational institutions.
