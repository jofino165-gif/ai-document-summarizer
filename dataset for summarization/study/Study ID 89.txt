Topic:
Cybersecurity

Uploaded Notes:
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, programs, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, damage, or theft. It ensures confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information (CIA triad).

Cybersecurity involves multiple layers of protection including network security, application security, information security, and operational security. It uses technologies, processes, and controls to defend against cyber threats.

Common cyber threats include malware, ransomware, phishing, spyware, denial-of-service (DoS) attacks, man-in-the-middle attacks, SQL injection, and zero-day vulnerabilities.

Key security mechanisms include firewalls, antivirus software, encryption, intrusion detection systems (IDS), intrusion prevention systems (IPS), multi-factor authentication (MFA), and secure protocols like HTTPS.

Cybersecurity domains include network security, cloud security, endpoint security, application security, and identity and access management (IAM).

Cybersecurity is widely used in banking systems, government organizations, healthcare systems, e-commerce platforms, defense systems, and personal devices.

The advantages of cybersecurity include data protection, risk reduction, regulatory compliance, system reliability, and protection against cybercrime. Challenges include evolving threats, skill shortages, high costs, and complex system environments.

Summary:
Cybersecurity focuses on protecting systems, networks, and data from cyber threats using layered defense mechanisms and security technologies.
