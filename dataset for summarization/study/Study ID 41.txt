Topic:
Internet of Things (IoT)

Uploaded Notes:
The Internet of Things (IoT) is a technology that connects physical devices, sensors, appliances, vehicles, and machines to the internet, enabling them to collect, exchange, and analyze data automatically. IoT allows devices to communicate with each other without human intervention, improving automation, monitoring, and decision-making across various industries.

An IoT system consists of sensors, actuators, microcontrollers, communication networks, cloud platforms, data processing units, and user applications. Sensors collect real-time data such as temperature, humidity, motion, pressure, and location, while actuators perform actions based on received commands. Data is transmitted through communication protocols such as MQTT, HTTP, CoAP, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, Zigbee, and LoRaWAN.

Popular IoT hardware platforms include Arduino, Raspberry Pi, ESP32, ESP8266, and BeagleBone. Cloud platforms such as AWS IoT, Microsoft Azure IoT, and Google Cloud IoT provide services for device management, analytics, and secure communication.

IoT is widely used in smart homes, smart cities, healthcare, agriculture, industrial automation, transportation, environmental monitoring, logistics, and wearable technology. It enables real-time monitoring, predictive maintenance, energy management, and remote device control.

The advantages of IoT include automation, improved efficiency, real-time monitoring, better resource utilization, enhanced decision-making, and cost savings. Challenges include cybersecurity risks, data privacy concerns, device interoperability, scalability, and network reliability.

Summary:
The Internet of Things (IoT) connects smart devices through the internet, enabling real-time data collection, communication, and automation. It plays a significant role in smart homes, healthcare, industry, agriculture, and transportation.
