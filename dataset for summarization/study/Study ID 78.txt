Topic:
Internet of Things (IoT)

Uploaded Notes:
The Internet of Things (IoT) is a technology that connects physical devices such as sensors, machines, appliances, and vehicles to the internet, enabling them to collect, share, and exchange data. IoT allows devices to communicate with each other and perform automated actions without human intervention.

IoT systems consist of four main components: devices (sensors/actuators), connectivity (networks like Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and 5G), data processing (cloud or edge computing), and user interface (applications and dashboards).

IoT architecture is typically divided into three layers: perception layer (sensors and data collection), network layer (data transmission), and application layer (user services and applications).

Common IoT communication protocols include MQTT, CoAP, HTTP, Zigbee, LoRaWAN, and Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE). These protocols ensure efficient and low-power communication between devices.

IoT is widely used in smart homes, smart cities, healthcare monitoring, agriculture, industrial automation, transportation, wearable devices, and energy management systems.

Examples of IoT devices include smart watches, smart thermostats, security cameras, connected cars, and industrial sensors.

The advantages of IoT include automation, real-time monitoring, improved efficiency, data-driven decision-making, and cost savings. Challenges include security risks, privacy concerns, interoperability issues, network reliability, and high implementation costs.

Summary:
IoT connects physical devices to the internet, enabling them to collect and exchange data for automation, monitoring, and intelligent decision-making.
