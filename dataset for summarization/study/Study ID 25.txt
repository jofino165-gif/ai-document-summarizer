Topic:
Kanban Methodology

Uploaded Notes:
Kanban is a visual workflow management methodology used in software development and project management to improve efficiency, productivity, and continuous delivery. It originated from the Toyota Production System and is widely used in Agile software development environments. Kanban focuses on visualizing work, limiting work in progress (WIP), and improving flow.

A Kanban system uses a Kanban board, which is divided into columns such as To Do, In Progress, and Done. Tasks or work items are represented as cards that move across the board as they progress through different stages. This helps teams easily track work status and identify bottlenecks.

The key principles of Kanban include visualizing workflow, limiting work in progress, managing flow, making process policies explicit, implementing feedback loops, and continuously improving processes. Unlike Scrum, Kanban does not require fixed time iterations, making it more flexible for ongoing tasks.

Kanban is widely used in software development, IT operations, manufacturing, project management, customer support, and service industries. It helps teams improve delivery speed, reduce delays, and increase efficiency.

The advantages of Kanban include improved workflow visibility, reduced task overload, continuous delivery, better team collaboration, and increased productivity. Challenges include lack of strict structure, difficulty in long-term planning, and dependency on team discipline.

Summary:
Kanban is a visual workflow management method that helps teams manage tasks efficiently by using boards, limiting work in progress, and continuously improving processes. It is widely used in Agile environments for software development and project management.
