Topic:
Blockchain Technology

Uploaded Notes:
Blockchain Technology is a decentralized digital ledger system that securely records transactions across multiple computers. Instead of storing data in a central database, blockchain distributes identical copies of the ledger across a network of nodes, ensuring transparency, security, and immutability. Once a block is added to the chain, its data cannot be easily modified, making blockchain highly resistant to fraud and unauthorized changes.

A blockchain consists of blocks, each containing transaction data, a timestamp, a cryptographic hash, and the hash of the previous block. These blocks are linked together chronologically, forming a secure chain. Consensus mechanisms such as Proof of Work (PoW), Proof of Stake (PoS), and Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS) ensure that all participants agree on the validity of transactions before they are added to the blockchain.

Smart contracts are self-executing programs stored on the blockchain that automatically enforce agreements when predefined conditions are met. Platforms such as Ethereum support smart contract development, enabling decentralized applications (DApps).

Blockchain technology is widely used in cryptocurrency, banking, healthcare, supply chain management, digital identity verification, voting systems, insurance, and asset management. It improves transparency, security, and traceability while reducing dependence on intermediaries.

The advantages of blockchain include decentralization, enhanced security, transparency, immutability, traceability, and reduced transaction costs. Challenges include scalability limitations, high energy consumption in some consensus mechanisms, regulatory uncertainty, storage requirements, and implementation complexity.

Summary:
Blockchain Technology is a decentralized digital ledger that securely records transactions using cryptographic techniques. It enables transparent, tamper-resistant, and trustless data management for cryptocurrencies, smart contracts, and decentralized applications.
