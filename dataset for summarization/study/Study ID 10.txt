Topic:
Internet of Things (IoT)

Uploaded Notes:
The Internet of Things (IoT) is a technology that connects physical devices such as sensors, appliances, vehicles, and machines to the internet, allowing them to collect, share, and exchange data. IoT enables smart communication between devices without requiring human intervention, making systems more efficient, automated, and intelligent.

IoT systems consist of four main components: devices (sensors and actuators), connectivity (networks such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or 5G), data processing (cloud or edge computing), and a user interface (mobile apps or dashboards). These components work together to collect data, process it, and provide useful insights or automated actions.

IoT is widely used in smart homes, smart cities, healthcare monitoring, agriculture, industrial automation, transportation systems, wearable devices, and energy management. Examples include smart thermostats, fitness trackers, smart traffic systems, and connected industrial machines.

The advantages of IoT include automation, real-time monitoring, improved efficiency, cost savings, better decision-making, and enhanced user convenience. However, challenges include data security risks, privacy concerns, network dependency, device compatibility issues, and high implementation costs.

Summary:
