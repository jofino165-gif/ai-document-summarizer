# Legal Document 25 – Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

### Original Text

A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is a legally binding contract between a data controller and a data processor that defines the terms under which personal data will be processed. The agreement is designed to ensure that personal information is handled securely, lawfully, and in compliance with applicable data protection regulations. Organizations commonly use Data Processing Agreements when outsourcing services such as cloud computing, payroll management, customer support, data analytics, information technology services, and digital marketing. The agreement establishes clear responsibilities for both parties and helps protect the privacy rights of individuals whose personal information is being processed.

The agreement begins by identifying the data controller and the data processor, including their legal names, business addresses, and contact information. It also specifies the effective date of the agreement and describes the purpose for which personal data will be processed. The agreement clearly identifies the categories of personal information involved, such as customer names, contact details, financial records, employee information, identification numbers, online account data, transaction histories, and technical system logs. Clearly defining the scope of processing helps ensure that both parties understand the nature of the personal information being handled.

One of the most important sections of the agreement outlines the responsibilities of the data processor. The processor agrees to process personal information only according to the documented instructions provided by the data controller. The processor must not use personal data for unauthorized purposes or disclose it to third parties without written approval unless required by law. The agreement also requires the processor to implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures, including encryption, secure authentication, access controls, firewall protection, regular software updates, and employee security training to safeguard personal information against unauthorized access or accidental loss.

The agreement also defines the responsibilities of the data controller. The controller is responsible for ensuring that personal data is collected lawfully, that appropriate consent has been obtained where required, and that individuals are informed about how their personal information will be used. The controller must provide accurate instructions to the processor and monitor compliance throughout the processing relationship.

Another important component of the agreement addresses the rights of individuals whose personal information is processed. The agreement requires both parties to cooperate when responding to requests for access, correction, deletion, restriction of processing, or data portability. If an individual exercises any of these rights, the processor must promptly assist the controller in fulfilling the request according to applicable legal requirements.

The agreement establishes procedures for managing personal data breaches. If the processor becomes aware of unauthorized access, accidental disclosure, data loss, or any security incident affecting personal information, the processor must immediately notify the data controller. Both parties agree to investigate the incident, implement corrective actions, minimize potential harm, and comply with any legal reporting obligations imposed by applicable privacy regulations.

Termination provisions explain how personal information must be handled when the agreement ends. Upon termination, the processor must either securely return all personal data to the controller or permanently delete it using approved data destruction methods, unless applicable law requires continued retention. Confidentiality obligations remain in effect even after the agreement has expired to ensure that sensitive information continues to be protected.

Finally, the Data Processing Agreement includes provisions regarding audits, compliance monitoring, governing law, dispute resolution, and legal jurisdiction. The controller may conduct periodic audits or request compliance reports to verify that the processor is fulfilling its contractual obligations. By clearly defining processing activities, security requirements, confidentiality obligations, breach notification procedures, individual rights, and legal responsibilities, a Data Processing Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that protects personal information, strengthens regulatory compliance, and promotes trust between organizations that process sensitive data.
