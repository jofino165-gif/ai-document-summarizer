# Legal Document 48 – Data Sharing Agreement

### Original Text

A Data Sharing Agreement is a legally binding contract between two or more organizations that establishes the terms and conditions for the exchange, access, use, storage, protection, and management of data. The agreement defines the rights, responsibilities, security requirements, confidentiality obligations, and legal restrictions applicable to all parties involved in the data-sharing arrangement. Data Sharing Agreements are commonly used between government agencies, healthcare organizations, research institutions, financial institutions, educational organizations, and private companies. The agreement promotes lawful and secure data exchange while protecting personal information, confidential business data, and intellectual property.

The agreement begins by identifying all participating parties, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the purpose of the data-sharing arrangement, and the categories of data that will be exchanged. These categories may include personal information, financial records, healthcare data, research datasets, customer information, business records, operational reports, or statistical information. Clearly defining the scope of the data exchange ensures that all parties understand what information may be shared and for what purposes.

One of the most important sections of the Data Sharing Agreement outlines the responsibilities of each participating party. The data provider agrees to supply accurate, complete, and lawfully obtained information, while the receiving party agrees to use the data only for the purposes specified in the agreement. The receiving party must not disclose, modify, copy, sell, or distribute the shared data to any unauthorized third party without obtaining prior written consent from the data provider. These obligations help maintain trust and ensure responsible data management.

The agreement also establishes comprehensive data security requirements. Each party agrees to implement appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect shared information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, loss, theft, or destruction. Security measures may include encryption, access controls, authentication mechanisms, secure network communications, regular security audits, employee training, and incident response procedures. If a data breach occurs, the affected party must promptly notify the other parties and cooperate in investigating and mitigating the incident.

Confidentiality provisions require all participating organizations to protect confidential information exchanged under the agreement. Confidential information may include customer records, financial information, medical records, research findings, software source code, trade secrets, technical documentation, business strategies, and proprietary databases. Confidential information may only be disclosed when required by law or with prior written approval from the disclosing party. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective even after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also addresses compliance with applicable privacy and data protection laws. Each party agrees to process personal information fairly, lawfully, and only for authorized purposes. The agreement may require compliance with national and international data protection regulations, including requirements for obtaining consent, protecting individual privacy rights, maintaining accurate records, and securely disposing of personal information when it is no longer required. Regular compliance reviews and audits may be conducted to verify adherence to legal obligations.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which the Data Sharing Agreement may be terminated. Reasons for termination may include breach of contract, unauthorized disclosure of data, failure to comply with applicable laws, insolvency, completion of the agreed project, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the receiving party must securely return or permanently destroy all shared data unless retention is required by law. Written confirmation of data destruction may also be required.

Finally, the Data Sharing Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, amendment procedures, audit rights, and legal compliance. If disagreements arise concerning the interpretation, implementation, or enforcement of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining data-sharing responsibilities, security requirements, confidentiality obligations, privacy compliance, termination procedures, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Data Sharing Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that enables secure, lawful, and efficient exchange of information while protecting the legal and commercial interests of all participating parties.
