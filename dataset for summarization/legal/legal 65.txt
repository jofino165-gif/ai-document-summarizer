# Legal Document 65 – Data Migration Agreement

### Original Text

A Data Migration Agreement is a legally binding contract between a client and a data migration service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for transferring data from one information system, database, application, or storage environment to another. The agreement defines the scope of migration services, responsibilities of both parties, migration procedures, security requirements, confidentiality obligations, payment terms, data validation processes, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Data Migration Agreements are commonly used during system upgrades, cloud migration projects, enterprise resource planning implementations, database modernization, mergers and acquisitions, and digital transformation initiatives. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that ensures secure, accurate, and efficient transfer of business information while protecting the legal and commercial interests of both parties.

The agreement begins by identifying the client and the data migration service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the migration project, and a detailed description of the source systems and destination systems involved in the migration process. The agreement identifies the categories of data to be transferred, including customer records, financial information, employee data, application data, databases, digital documents, configuration files, and business reports. Clearly defining the migration scope helps establish common expectations and minimizes misunderstandings during project execution.

One of the most important sections of the Data Migration Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the service provider. The provider agrees to analyze the existing data environment, prepare migration plans, perform data extraction, transform information into compatible formats where necessary, validate data accuracy, and load the information into the destination system. The provider is responsible for minimizing downtime, maintaining data integrity, documenting migration activities, resolving technical issues, and conducting post-migration verification to ensure successful completion of the project. Any migration errors identified during testing must be corrected promptly.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to provide authorized access to source systems, supply accurate documentation regarding existing databases and applications, identify critical business information, review migration plans, participate in testing activities, and approve the final migration before production deployment. The client is responsible for ensuring that data selected for migration is lawful, complete, and appropriately classified according to organizational policies. Cooperation between both parties is essential to achieve a successful migration with minimal disruption to business operations.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies project fees, milestone-based payments, billing procedures, payment schedules, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and charges for additional services beyond the agreed project scope. Additional services may include data cleansing, custom data transformation, application reconfiguration, extended technical support, or migration of additional systems not originally included in the agreement. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps prevent payment disputes and supports transparent project management.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the migration project. Confidential information may include customer records, employee information, financial reports, healthcare data, software source code, technical documentation, business strategies, database schemas, authentication credentials, encryption keys, and proprietary business processes. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally continue after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes security and data validation requirements. The service provider agrees to implement appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards including encryption, secure communication channels, access controls, authentication mechanisms, audit logging, and backup procedures throughout the migration process. Before final acceptance, both parties agree to perform data validation to verify that all records have been transferred accurately, completely, and without unauthorized modification. Any discrepancies identified during validation must be investigated and corrected before project completion.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Data Migration Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet agreed project milestones, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the service provider agrees to return or securely destroy all client-owned data, discontinue migration activities, remove temporary copies of migrated information where legally permitted, and provide all completed project documentation to the client.

Finally, the Data Migration Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, and amendment procedures. If disputes arise concerning migration services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, data accuracy, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining migration responsibilities, project procedures, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, security measures, data validation processes, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Data Migration Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports secure and reliable transfer of organizational data while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the data migration service provider.
