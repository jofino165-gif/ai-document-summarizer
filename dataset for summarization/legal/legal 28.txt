# Legal Document 28 – Escrow Agreement

### Original Text

An Escrow Agreement is a legally binding contract between two or more parties in which a neutral third party, known as the escrow agent, temporarily holds money, documents, property, or other valuable assets until specific contractual conditions have been fulfilled. Escrow Agreements are commonly used in real estate transactions, business acquisitions, software licensing, mergers and acquisitions, online commerce, intellectual property transfers, and financial transactions. The primary purpose of the agreement is to protect the interests of all parties by ensuring that assets are transferred only after the agreed obligations have been successfully completed.

The agreement begins by identifying the buyer, seller, and escrow agent, including their legal names, addresses, and contact information. It specifies the purpose of the escrow arrangement, describes the assets being held, and explains the conditions that must be satisfied before the escrow agent is authorized to release the assets. Clearly identifying the parties and the escrow property helps prevent misunderstandings and establishes a transparent legal relationship.

One of the most important sections of the Escrow Agreement defines the responsibilities of the escrow agent. The escrow agent is required to remain neutral and act strictly according to the instructions provided in the agreement. The agent must securely safeguard the deposited funds, documents, software source code, property titles, or other assets without favoring either party. The escrow agent is not permitted to release the escrow assets until all contractual conditions have been satisfied or until both parties provide written authorization.

The agreement also outlines the obligations of the buyer and the seller. The buyer agrees to deposit the agreed purchase amount into the escrow account within the specified timeframe, while the seller agrees to deliver the required goods, services, legal documents, or property according to the contractual terms. The agreement establishes clear deadlines for completing these obligations and describes the evidence that must be provided before the escrow agent may release the assets.

Confidentiality provisions require all parties, including the escrow agent, to protect confidential business information, financial records, customer data, software source code, technical documentation, and transaction details obtained during the escrow process. Confidential information may not be disclosed to unauthorized individuals unless disclosure is required by law or approved in writing by the parties involved. These confidentiality obligations help maintain trust throughout the transaction.

The Escrow Agreement also includes provisions for handling disputes. If disagreements arise regarding whether the contractual conditions have been satisfied, the escrow agent must continue holding the assets until the dispute has been resolved through mutual written instructions, arbitration, mediation, or a court order. This approach prevents either party from obtaining the assets before the dispute has been properly resolved.

The agreement specifies the fees payable to the escrow agent for providing escrow services. It explains how the fees will be calculated, which party will be responsible for payment, and the schedule for making those payments. Additional administrative costs or expenses incurred during the escrow process may also be allocated according to the terms of the agreement.

Termination provisions describe the circumstances under which the escrow arrangement may end. The agreement may terminate after successful completion of the transaction, mutual written consent of all parties, cancellation of the underlying transaction, or a final court order directing the release of the escrow assets. Upon termination, the escrow agent must distribute the assets according to the instructions provided in the agreement or applicable legal authority.

Finally, the Escrow Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, and legal compliance. If legal disputes arise concerning the interpretation or enforcement of the agreement, the parties agree to attempt negotiation or mediation before initiating arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining the responsibilities of the escrow agent, the obligations of the parties, confidentiality requirements, dispute handling procedures, payment of escrow fees, and termination conditions, an Escrow Agreement provides a secure legal framework that protects all participants and ensures the fair completion of important financial and commercial transactions.
