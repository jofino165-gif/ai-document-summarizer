# Legal Document 34 – Copyright License Agreement

### Original Text

A Copyright License Agreement is a legally binding contract between the owner of a copyrighted work, known as the licensor, and another party, known as the licensee, who is granted permission to use the copyrighted material under specified terms and conditions. The agreement does not transfer ownership of the copyright but instead grants limited rights to use, reproduce, distribute, display, or modify the protected work for agreed purposes. Copyright License Agreements are commonly used for books, software, photographs, videos, music, research publications, educational materials, digital content, and artistic works. The agreement helps protect the intellectual property rights of the copyright owner while allowing lawful use by the licensee.

The agreement begins by identifying the licensor and the licensee, including their legal names, addresses, and contact information. It specifies the effective date of the agreement and provides a detailed description of the copyrighted work covered by the license. This description may include the title of the work, registration number, publication date, version number, and other identifying information. Clearly identifying the copyrighted material helps prevent misunderstandings regarding the scope of the license.

One of the most important sections of the agreement defines the license being granted. The agreement specifies whether the license is exclusive or non-exclusive, transferable or non-transferable, and whether the licensee has permission to reproduce, distribute, publish, modify, perform, or display the copyrighted work. The agreement may also restrict the use of the work to specific geographic regions, industries, languages, or periods of time. Clearly defining the scope of the license helps protect the interests of both parties.

The agreement also outlines the financial terms associated with the license. The licensee agrees to pay licensing fees, royalties, subscription charges, or other agreed compensation in exchange for the rights granted under the agreement. The document specifies payment schedules, methods of payment, applicable taxes, and the consequences of late or missed payments. Some agreements require periodic royalty reports based on sales or usage of the licensed work.

Confidentiality provisions protect any proprietary information exchanged during the licensing relationship. Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of unpublished materials, business strategies, financial records, technical information, customer data, and licensing terms unless disclosure is required by law or approved in writing. These obligations generally remain effective even after the agreement has ended.

The agreement also establishes the responsibilities of the licensee. The licensee agrees to use the copyrighted work only in accordance with the rights granted under the agreement and not to infringe the licensor's ownership rights. Unauthorized copying, resale, sublicensing, or modification beyond the permitted scope may constitute copyright infringement and result in legal action. The licensee must also retain all copyright notices and ownership acknowledgments included with the licensed material.

Termination provisions describe the circumstances under which the agreement may end. Reasons for termination may include breach of contract, non-payment of licensing fees, unauthorized use of the copyrighted work, bankruptcy, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the licensee must immediately stop using the copyrighted material, destroy or return unauthorized copies where required, and fulfill any remaining financial obligations under the agreement.

Finally, the Copyright License Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, and legal compliance. If disputes arise concerning the interpretation, enforcement, or scope of the license, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining licensing rights, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, permitted uses, termination conditions, and dispute resolution procedures, a Copyright License Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that protects intellectual property while enabling the lawful use of copyrighted works by authorized parties.
