# Legal Document 90 – Software Licensing Agreement

### Original Text

A Software Licensing Agreement is a legally binding contract between a software licensor and a licensee that establishes the terms and conditions under which software may be installed, accessed, used, distributed, and maintained. The agreement defines the scope of the software license, permitted and prohibited uses, intellectual property rights, payment terms, maintenance and support provisions, confidentiality obligations, warranty limitations, liability clauses, termination conditions, compliance requirements, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Software Licensing Agreements are commonly used by software companies, technology vendors, businesses, educational institutions, healthcare organizations, financial institutions, government agencies, and individual users to ensure lawful software usage while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the licensor and the licensee. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that governs software ownership and usage throughout the license period.

The agreement begins by identifying the software licensor and the licensee, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives where applicable. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the license, and a detailed description of the licensed software, including the software name, version, supported platforms, documentation, associated modules, and any related services covered under the agreement. The agreement also specifies the type of license granted, which may include perpetual, subscription-based, single-user, multi-user, enterprise, or limited-use licensing. Clearly defining the scope of the license helps establish expectations and reduces misunderstandings regarding software usage rights.

One of the most important sections of the Software Licensing Agreement outlines the rights and responsibilities of the licensee. The licensee is granted a non-exclusive, non-transferable, and revocable license to install and use the software solely for the purposes permitted under the agreement. The licensee agrees not to copy, modify, reverse engineer, decompile, sublicense, lease, sell, distribute, or otherwise exploit the software except as expressly authorized by the licensor or permitted by applicable law. The licensee is responsible for protecting license keys, restricting unauthorized access, maintaining software security, and ensuring compliance with all license terms.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the software licensor. The licensor agrees to provide the software, associated documentation, and any maintenance or technical support services included under the license. The licensor is responsible for delivering software updates, security patches, and bug fixes where applicable, maintaining the confidentiality of customer information, and providing reasonable technical assistance during the support period. The licensor also warrants that the software has the legal authority to be licensed and does not knowingly infringe the intellectual property rights of third parties.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies licensing fees, subscription charges where applicable, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, renewal fees, reimbursement of approved expenses, and charges for optional services such as premium support, additional user licenses, software customization, training, or consulting services. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and promotes transparent commercial relationships.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the licensing relationship. Confidential information may include software source code, technical documentation, license keys, authentication credentials, customer records, financial information, business strategies, proprietary algorithms, trade secrets, research information, and intellectual property. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes intellectual property ownership and compliance requirements. The licensor retains full ownership of the software, source code, trademarks, copyrights, patents, documentation, and all associated intellectual property rights. The license granted under the agreement does not transfer ownership of the software to the licensee. The licensor may conduct reasonable license compliance audits to verify that the software is being used in accordance with the agreement, subject to reasonable notice and applicable legal requirements.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Software Licensing Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, unauthorized software use, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the licensee agrees to discontinue all use of the software, uninstall or destroy all software copies where required, return confidential information, and certify compliance with the termination requirements if requested. The licensor agrees to discontinue maintenance services where applicable following termination.

Finally, the Software Licensing Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, amendment procedures, warranty disclaimers, and force majeure. If disputes arise concerning software licensing rights, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property ownership, software usage restrictions, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining software licensing rights, user responsibilities, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property protections, compliance procedures, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Software Licensing Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports lawful software usage while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the software licensor and the licensee.
