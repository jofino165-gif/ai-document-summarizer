# Legal Document 98 – AI Software as a Service (SaaS) Agreement

### Original Text

An AI Software as a Service (SaaS) Agreement is a legally binding contract between a software provider and a customer that establishes the terms and conditions for accessing and using cloud-based artificial intelligence software through a subscription model. The agreement defines the scope of AI SaaS services, user access rights, subscription plans, service level commitments, data processing responsibilities, security requirements, confidentiality obligations, payment terms, intellectual property rights, compliance requirements, availability guarantees, and dispute resolution mechanisms. AI SaaS Agreements are commonly used by businesses, healthcare organizations, financial institutions, educational institutions, government agencies, startups, and technology enterprises to securely access AI-powered applications such as predictive analytics platforms, natural language processing tools, computer vision services, recommendation engines, intelligent automation systems, and conversational AI solutions. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that supports reliable cloud-based AI services while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both parties.

The agreement begins by identifying the AI software provider and the customer, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives where applicable. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the subscription period, and a detailed description of the AI software services covered under the agreement. The agreement identifies subscription plans, authorized users, supported platforms, service availability commitments, application programming interfaces (APIs), usage limits, storage capacity, maintenance windows, and customer support services. Clearly defining the subscription scope helps establish service expectations and minimizes misunderstandings regarding access rights and operational responsibilities.

One of the most important sections of the AI SaaS Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the software provider. The provider agrees to host and maintain the AI platform, ensure service availability according to agreed service levels, implement security controls, perform regular software updates, provide technical support, monitor system performance, maintain backup systems, protect customer data, and promptly address service interruptions. The provider is responsible for maintaining operational documentation, monitoring infrastructure performance, implementing cybersecurity measures, and notifying customers of significant service changes or scheduled maintenance activities.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the customer. The customer agrees to use the AI software only for authorized purposes, maintain account security, protect user credentials, provide accurate subscription information, comply with usage policies, promptly report service issues, and make payments according to the agreed subscription schedule. The customer is responsible for ensuring that uploaded data has been lawfully collected, complying with applicable data protection regulations, managing user access permissions, and using AI-generated outputs responsibly. Effective cooperation between both parties contributes to reliable and secure service delivery.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies subscription fees, recurring billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, renewal procedures, reimbursement of approved expenses where applicable, and charges for additional services beyond the agreed subscription plan. Additional services may include expanded storage, premium technical support, custom AI model integration, advanced analytics features, additional API usage, enterprise onboarding, or user training. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce billing disputes and supports transparent commercial relationships.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the SaaS relationship. Confidential information may include customer data, AI models, software source code, authentication credentials, technical documentation, financial information, business strategies, proprietary algorithms, research information, intellectual property, and trade secrets. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes data processing, service availability, and compliance requirements. The software provider agrees to implement appropriate encryption, access controls, monitoring systems, backup procedures, disaster recovery capabilities, and periodic security assessments to protect customer information. Both parties agree to comply with applicable laws governing privacy, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, intellectual property, and electronic communications. The provider also agrees to maintain agreed service availability levels and communicate planned maintenance activities in advance whenever reasonably possible.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the AI SaaS Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, non-payment, unauthorized system access, violation of confidentiality obligations, unlawful use of the platform, insolvency, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the customer agrees to discontinue use of the service, while the provider agrees to provide data export options where applicable, securely delete retained customer information according to applicable laws, discontinue service access, and provide reasonable transition assistance if specified in the agreement.

Finally, the AI SaaS Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, audit rights, amendment procedures, service reporting, compliance reviews, force majeure, and periodic subscription evaluations. If disputes arise concerning subscription services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property ownership, service availability, data processing activities, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining subscription rights, service responsibilities, financial obligations, confidentiality protections, data security requirements, service level commitments, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, an AI Software as a Service (SaaS) Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports secure, reliable, and compliant delivery of cloud-based artificial intelligence services while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the software provider and the customer.
