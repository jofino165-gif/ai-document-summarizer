# Legal Document 55 – Service Level Agreement (SLA)

### Original Text

A Service Level Agreement (SLA) is a legally binding contract between a service provider and a customer that defines the expected level of service to be delivered throughout the duration of a business relationship. The agreement establishes measurable performance standards, service availability requirements, response and resolution times, maintenance responsibilities, reporting obligations, payment terms, confidentiality provisions, and dispute resolution procedures. Service Level Agreements are commonly used in information technology services, cloud computing, internet services, managed services, telecommunications, customer support, software maintenance, and business process outsourcing. The agreement helps ensure that both parties clearly understand the quality standards and operational expectations associated with the services being provided.

The agreement begins by identifying the service provider and the customer, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the service relationship, and a detailed description of the services covered by the SLA. The agreement may include technical support services, network management, software maintenance, cloud hosting, cybersecurity monitoring, help desk support, or infrastructure management. Clearly defining the scope of services helps prevent misunderstandings regarding the provider's responsibilities.

One of the most important sections of the Service Level Agreement establishes the service performance standards. The provider agrees to maintain specified service availability, commonly measured as system uptime percentages, response times, resolution times, and service quality indicators. The agreement categorizes incidents based on their severity and assigns corresponding response and resolution targets. Critical service interruptions receive immediate attention, while lower-priority issues are addressed according to predefined service schedules. Performance metrics are monitored regularly to ensure compliance with agreed service standards.

The agreement also outlines the responsibilities of the service provider. The provider agrees to maintain qualified technical personnel, perform preventive maintenance, monitor service performance continuously, implement security controls, provide regular software updates, and notify the customer of scheduled maintenance activities. The provider is also responsible for maintaining accurate service records, preparing periodic performance reports, and responding promptly to customer support requests. These responsibilities help ensure consistent and reliable service delivery.

The agreement specifies the responsibilities of the customer as well. The customer agrees to use the services in accordance with the agreement, provide accurate information when reporting service issues, maintain authorized access credentials securely, cooperate during troubleshooting activities, and comply with all applicable usage policies. The customer is also responsible for providing timely feedback, reviewing service reports, and making payments according to the agreed billing schedule. Effective cooperation between both parties contributes to successful service management.

Financial provisions define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies subscription fees, service charges, billing cycles, payment methods, applicable taxes, and penalties for delayed payments. The agreement may also include service credits or financial compensation if the provider fails to achieve the agreed performance levels. These provisions encourage accountability and provide remedies for service deficiencies.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the service relationship. Confidential information may include customer records, financial information, technical documentation, system configurations, software source code, security procedures, business strategies, and proprietary operational data. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally continue after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Service Level Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet service performance standards, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the provider agrees to discontinue services in an orderly manner, return or securely destroy confidential information where appropriate, and settle any outstanding contractual obligations.

Finally, the Service Level Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, amendment procedures, limitation of liability, force majeure, and legal compliance. If disputes arise concerning service performance, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, or contractual interpretation, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining service standards, provider responsibilities, customer obligations, financial arrangements, confidentiality requirements, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Service Level Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports reliable service delivery while protecting the legal and commercial interests of both the service provider and the customer.
