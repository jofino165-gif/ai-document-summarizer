# Legal Document 76 – Information Security Policy Agreement

### Original Text

An Information Security Policy Agreement is a legally binding contract between an organization and its employees, contractors, consultants, or authorized users that establishes the rules, responsibilities, and security requirements for protecting organizational information assets. The agreement defines acceptable use policies, access control requirements, confidentiality obligations, data protection measures, cybersecurity responsibilities, compliance requirements, incident reporting procedures, disciplinary actions, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Information Security Policy Agreements are commonly used by businesses, financial institutions, healthcare organizations, educational institutions, government agencies, and technology companies to safeguard sensitive information and ensure compliance with applicable information security laws and industry standards. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that promotes responsible information security practices while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of the organization.

The agreement begins by identifying the organization and the authorized individual entering into the agreement, including their legal names, business addresses, job titles, and authorized representatives where applicable. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the security obligations, and the categories of information systems, networks, applications, cloud services, databases, and digital resources covered under the policy. The agreement also defines the classification of organizational information, including public, internal, confidential, and highly confidential information. Clearly defining the scope of the policy helps establish security expectations and reduces misunderstandings regarding user responsibilities.

One of the most important sections of the Information Security Policy Agreement outlines the responsibilities of authorized users. Users agree to protect organizational information assets by using strong passwords, enabling multi-factor authentication where required, safeguarding authentication credentials, locking unattended devices, complying with access control policies, and using organizational systems only for authorized business purposes. Users are prohibited from sharing passwords, installing unauthorized software, bypassing security controls, or engaging in activities that may compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of organizational information systems.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the organization. The organization agrees to implement appropriate administrative, technical, and physical security safeguards to protect information assets from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, destruction, or loss. These safeguards may include firewalls, encryption, endpoint protection, security monitoring, access management systems, backup procedures, disaster recovery planning, vulnerability management, and employee security awareness training. The organization is also responsible for maintaining security policies, providing periodic security training, and responding promptly to reported security incidents.

Financial provisions, where applicable, define responsibilities relating to organizational equipment, reimbursement for damaged assets resulting from unauthorized use, recovery of investigation costs arising from intentional policy violations, and payment obligations associated with security training or certification programs. The agreement may also specify financial liabilities resulting from deliberate misuse of organizational information systems where permitted by applicable law.

Confidentiality provisions require authorized users to protect confidential information obtained during employment or contractual engagements. Confidential information may include customer records, employee information, financial reports, healthcare records, software source code, technical documentation, network configurations, authentication credentials, encryption keys, trade secrets, business strategies, and proprietary technologies. Authorized users agree not to disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written approval unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally continue after employment or contractual relationships have ended.

The agreement also establishes procedures for reporting and responding to security incidents. Authorized users agree to immediately report suspected phishing attempts, malware infections, unauthorized access, data breaches, lost devices, suspicious activities, or other cybersecurity incidents to the designated security team. The organization agrees to investigate reported incidents promptly, implement corrective actions, preserve relevant evidence where appropriate, and maintain records of incident response activities in accordance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which access privileges may be suspended or revoked. Reasons may include violation of security policies, unauthorized access to information systems, misuse of organizational resources, non-compliance with security requirements, termination of employment or contractual relationships, or other circumstances specified in the agreement. Upon termination, authorized users agree to return organizational equipment, surrender access credentials, return confidential information, and permanently delete organizational data stored on personal devices where authorized by organizational policy.

Finally, the Information Security Policy Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, legal compliance, audit rights, policy amendments, and periodic security reviews. If disputes arise concerning policy violations, confidentiality obligations, security responsibilities, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or internal resolution before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings where appropriate. By clearly defining user responsibilities, organizational security obligations, confidentiality requirements, incident reporting procedures, access control measures, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, an Information Security Policy Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports effective protection of organizational information assets while safeguarding the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the organization and its authorized users.
