# Legal Document 56 – Software License Agreement

### Original Text

A Software License Agreement is a legally binding contract between a software developer or software vendor and a customer that establishes the terms and conditions governing the installation, access, and use of software. The agreement defines the scope of the software license, permitted uses, usage restrictions, intellectual property rights, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, warranty provisions, and dispute resolution procedures. Software License Agreements are commonly used for desktop applications, enterprise software, mobile applications, cloud-based platforms, business management systems, educational software, financial software, and other digital products. The agreement protects the intellectual property of the software developer while granting the customer a lawful right to use the software under specified conditions.

The agreement begins by identifying the software vendor and the customer, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement and provides a detailed description of the licensed software, including its name, version, supported operating systems, technical specifications, and documentation. The agreement also identifies the type of license being granted, such as a perpetual license, subscription-based license, single-user license, multi-user license, enterprise license, or evaluation license. Clearly defining the software and license type helps prevent misunderstandings regarding the customer's rights and obligations.

One of the most important sections of the Software License Agreement outlines the license granted to the customer. The software vendor grants the customer a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, and revocable license to install and use the software solely for the purposes authorized under the agreement. The customer may use the software only within the permitted number of users, devices, or installations specified in the license. The agreement prohibits unauthorized copying, resale, sublicensing, reverse engineering, modification, distribution, or commercial exploitation of the software unless expressly authorized in writing by the software vendor.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the customer. The customer agrees to use the software in compliance with applicable laws and the terms of the agreement. The customer is responsible for protecting license keys, user credentials, activation codes, and other authentication mechanisms from unauthorized access. The customer must not use the software to engage in unlawful activities, introduce malicious code, or interfere with the normal operation of the software or related systems. Compliance with these responsibilities helps maintain software security and legal compliance.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies license fees, subscription charges where applicable, payment schedules, billing procedures, taxes, renewal terms, and penalties for delayed payments. The agreement may also describe maintenance fees, upgrade charges, and optional technical support services available to the customer. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports transparent business relationships.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the licensing relationship. Confidential information may include software source code, technical documentation, licensing information, security mechanisms, pricing details, customer records, business strategies, and proprietary technologies. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally continue after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also addresses intellectual property ownership. The software vendor retains full ownership of the software, source code, trademarks, copyrights, patents, documentation, and all related intellectual property rights. The customer receives only the right to use the software according to the terms of the license and does not acquire ownership of the software or any associated intellectual property. Any improvements, modifications, or derivative works created by the vendor remain the exclusive property of the vendor unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which the Software License Agreement may be terminated. Reasons for termination may include breach of contract, unauthorized software distribution, non-payment of license fees, violation of usage restrictions, insolvency, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the customer must immediately stop using the software, uninstall all copies, destroy or return confidential materials where required, and certify compliance with the termination obligations if requested by the software vendor.

Finally, the Software License Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, warranty disclaimers, legal compliance, and audit rights. If disputes arise concerning software usage, payment obligations, intellectual property rights, licensing restrictions, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining license rights, customer responsibilities, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property protections, termination procedures, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Software License Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports the lawful use of software while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the software vendor and the customer.
