# Legal Document 91 – Artificial Intelligence Development Agreement

### Original Text

An Artificial Intelligence Development Agreement is a legally binding contract between a client and an artificial intelligence development service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for designing, developing, training, testing, deploying, and maintaining artificial intelligence solutions. The agreement defines the scope of AI development services, responsibilities of both parties, project deliverables, data usage requirements, intellectual property rights, confidentiality obligations, payment terms, compliance requirements, model performance expectations, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Artificial Intelligence Development Agreements are commonly used by businesses, healthcare organizations, financial institutions, educational institutions, government agencies, manufacturing companies, and technology enterprises to develop AI-powered applications while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both parties. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that supports responsible AI development and successful project delivery.

The agreement begins by identifying the client and the AI development service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the project, and a detailed description of the artificial intelligence solution to be developed. The project may include machine learning models, natural language processing systems, computer vision applications, recommendation engines, predictive analytics solutions, intelligent automation tools, or conversational AI systems. The agreement identifies project objectives, datasets to be used, development methodologies, model evaluation criteria, expected deliverables, supported platforms, and implementation timelines. Clearly defining the project scope helps establish mutual expectations and minimizes misunderstandings during the AI development lifecycle.

One of the most important sections of the Artificial Intelligence Development Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the AI development service provider. The provider agrees to design AI models, preprocess training data, develop algorithms, train and validate models, evaluate model performance, document technical processes, deploy approved AI solutions, and provide technical support according to the agreed project requirements. The provider is responsible for implementing appropriate security measures, following recognized software engineering and machine learning best practices, monitoring model performance after deployment where applicable, and addressing identified defects or performance issues within agreed response times.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to provide accurate project requirements, authorized datasets, technical documentation, business objectives, testing feedback, and timely approvals required for successful project execution. The client is responsible for ensuring that provided datasets are lawfully obtained, maintaining compliance with applicable data protection laws, reviewing AI model outputs, participating in acceptance testing, and making payments according to the agreed financial schedule. Effective collaboration between both parties contributes to successful AI implementation.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies project fees, milestone-based payments, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and charges for additional services beyond the agreed scope. Additional services may include model retraining, cloud deployment, dataset expansion, feature engineering, performance optimization, user training, maintenance services, or integration with third-party systems. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports transparent project management.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the AI development engagement. Confidential information may include training datasets, software source code, model architectures, algorithms, customer records, employee information, financial reports, authentication credentials, encryption keys, technical documentation, research information, business strategies, proprietary technologies, and intellectual property. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes intellectual property ownership and ethical AI requirements. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the client retains ownership of project-specific deliverables, trained AI models, datasets provided by the client, and related documentation upon full payment. The service provider retains ownership of pre-existing algorithms, software libraries, development frameworks, reusable machine learning components, and independently developed technologies unless specifically assigned under the agreement. Both parties agree to comply with applicable laws governing privacy, data protection, intellectual property, fairness, transparency, accountability, and ethical use of artificial intelligence technologies throughout the project lifecycle.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Artificial Intelligence Development Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet agreed project milestones, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, unlawful use of datasets, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the service provider agrees to deliver completed project work, return confidential information, transfer project documentation where applicable, securely delete client-provided datasets where legally permitted, and provide reasonable transition assistance according to the agreement.

Finally, the Artificial Intelligence Development Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, amendment procedures, change request management, performance evaluation, and periodic project reviews. If disputes arise concerning AI development services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property ownership, dataset usage, model performance, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining AI development responsibilities, project deliverables, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property protections, ethical AI principles, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, an Artificial Intelligence Development Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports responsible and effective AI solution development while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the AI development service provider.
