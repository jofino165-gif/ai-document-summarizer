# Legal Document 74 – Security Incident Response Agreement

### Original Text

A Security Incident Response Agreement is a legally binding contract between a client and a security incident response service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for responding to, managing, investigating, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents affecting the client's information systems, networks, applications, and digital assets. The agreement defines the scope of incident response services, responsibilities of both parties, response procedures, communication protocols, confidentiality obligations, payment terms, regulatory compliance requirements, reporting procedures, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Security Incident Response Agreements are commonly used by businesses, financial institutions, healthcare organizations, educational institutions, government agencies, cloud service providers, and technology companies to ensure timely and effective responses to cybersecurity incidents while minimizing operational disruption and financial losses. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that supports coordinated incident management while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both parties.

The agreement begins by identifying the client and the security incident response service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the engagement, and a detailed description of the systems, applications, cloud environments, databases, communication networks, and digital assets covered under the agreement. The agreement also identifies the categories of incidents covered, including malware infections, ransomware attacks, unauthorized access, data breaches, denial-of-service attacks, insider threats, phishing incidents, and other cybersecurity events. Clearly defining the scope of incident response services helps establish expectations and ensures effective coordination during security emergencies.

One of the most important sections of the Security Incident Response Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the service provider. The provider agrees to investigate reported security incidents, identify the source and scope of the incident, contain ongoing threats, preserve digital evidence, perform forensic analysis where necessary, eliminate malicious activities, restore affected systems, and provide recommendations to prevent similar incidents in the future. The provider is responsible for maintaining qualified incident response personnel, documenting all response activities, preparing incident reports, and communicating regularly with the client throughout the incident management process.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to report suspected security incidents promptly, provide authorized access to affected systems, preserve relevant system logs and evidence where possible, cooperate during investigations, and implement recommended corrective actions. The client is responsible for notifying internal stakeholders, maintaining current system backups, complying with applicable legal reporting requirements, and ensuring that employees cooperate fully during incident response activities. Effective communication between both parties contributes to timely containment and recovery.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies incident response service fees, emergency response charges, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and additional charges for specialized forensic investigations, extended recovery operations, legal consultation, regulatory reporting assistance, or post-incident security assessments. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports transparent service delivery.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during incident response activities. Confidential information may include security logs, forensic reports, customer records, employee information, financial data, authentication credentials, software source code, technical documentation, network diagrams, encryption keys, and proprietary business information. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes incident reporting and regulatory compliance procedures. If a cybersecurity incident involves unauthorized disclosure of personal information or other legally protected data, both parties agree to cooperate in fulfilling applicable regulatory notification requirements within the prescribed legal timeframes. The service provider agrees to prepare incident documentation, assist with regulatory investigations where appropriate, and support post-incident reviews to identify lessons learned and improve future incident response capabilities.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Security Incident Response Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet agreed response obligations, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the service provider agrees to return confidential information, securely delete retained investigation data where legally permitted, discontinue response services, and provide all completed reports and documentation to the client according to the agreement.

Finally, the Security Incident Response Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, amendment procedures, and service review requirements. If disputes arise concerning incident response services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, regulatory compliance, investigation findings, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining incident response responsibilities, service standards, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, reporting procedures, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Security Incident Response Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports efficient management of cybersecurity incidents while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the security incident response service provider.
