# Legal Document 70 – Cloud Computing Services Agreement

### Original Text

A Cloud Computing Services Agreement is a legally binding contract between a cloud service provider and a client that establishes the terms and conditions for delivering cloud-based computing resources and related services. The agreement defines the scope of cloud services, responsibilities of both parties, service level commitments, payment terms, data security requirements, confidentiality obligations, intellectual property rights, service availability standards, and dispute resolution procedures. Cloud Computing Services Agreements are commonly used for infrastructure as a service (IaaS), platform as a service (PaaS), software as a service (SaaS), cloud storage, data hosting, application deployment, disaster recovery, and enterprise cloud solutions. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that ensures reliable cloud service delivery while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the cloud service provider.

The agreement begins by identifying the cloud service provider and the client, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the services, and a detailed description of the cloud resources covered under the contract. These resources may include virtual servers, cloud storage, databases, networking services, application hosting, backup systems, container platforms, artificial intelligence services, and monitoring tools. Clearly defining the cloud services helps establish common expectations and minimizes misunderstandings regarding the scope of service.

One of the most important sections of the Cloud Computing Services Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the cloud service provider. The provider agrees to maintain the cloud infrastructure, ensure agreed service availability, monitor system performance, implement security controls, install software updates, perform preventive maintenance, manage backup procedures, and respond promptly to service interruptions. The provider is responsible for protecting customer data, maintaining infrastructure reliability, monitoring resource utilization, and providing technical support according to the agreed service levels. Regular maintenance and monitoring activities help ensure consistent service quality.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to use the cloud services in compliance with applicable laws and the terms of the agreement. The client is responsible for maintaining secure user credentials, managing authorized access to cloud resources, protecting confidential information uploaded to the cloud environment, complying with acceptable use policies, and promptly reporting any suspected security incidents or service issues. The client must not use the cloud platform for unlawful activities or actions that could compromise the security or stability of the provider’s infrastructure.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies subscription fees, usage-based charges, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and additional fees for optional services such as expanded storage capacity, premium technical support, dedicated infrastructure, or advanced security features. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and promotes a transparent business relationship.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the cloud service relationship. Confidential information may include customer records, financial information, software source code, technical documentation, authentication credentials, encryption keys, business strategies, application data, system configurations, and proprietary technologies. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective even after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes data security and business continuity requirements. The cloud service provider agrees to implement appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards including encryption, firewalls, intrusion detection systems, multi-factor authentication, access controls, backup procedures, disaster recovery plans, and continuous security monitoring. In the event of hardware failures, cyberattacks, or service disruptions, the provider agrees to restore cloud services according to the agreed recovery objectives while minimizing downtime and protecting customer data.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Cloud Computing Services Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet agreed service levels, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the provider agrees to provide the client with reasonable access to retrieve stored data, securely remove retained customer information where legally permitted, discontinue cloud services, and complete an orderly transition according to the terms of the agreement.

Finally, the Cloud Computing Services Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, amendment procedures, and service level review processes. If disputes arise concerning cloud services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, security responsibilities, service availability, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining cloud service responsibilities, service standards, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, security measures, termination procedures, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Cloud Computing Services Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports secure, reliable, and efficient cloud service delivery while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the cloud service provider.
