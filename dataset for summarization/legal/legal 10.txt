## Legal Document 10 – Software License Agreement

### Original Text

A Software License Agreement is a legally binding contract between a software developer or software company, known as the licensor, and the individual or organization using the software, known as the licensee. The purpose of the agreement is to define the terms and conditions under which the software may be installed, accessed, and used. It protects the intellectual property rights of the software owner while granting the user permission to utilize the software within specified limits. Software License Agreements are commonly used for commercial software, mobile applications, enterprise systems, and cloud-based services.

The agreement begins by identifying the licensor and the licensee and providing details about the software product covered by the license. This may include the software name, version number, license key, and supported operating systems. The document also specifies the effective date of the agreement and confirms that the user accepts the license terms by installing or using the software.

One of the most important sections of the agreement defines the type of license being granted. The license may be a single-user license, multi-user license, enterprise license, educational license, or subscription-based license. The agreement specifies the number of devices or users permitted to access the software and whether the license is transferable to another individual or organization. Clearly defining the scope of the license helps prevent unauthorized use and protects the software owner's rights.

The agreement outlines the rights and responsibilities of the licensee. Users are generally permitted to install and use the software only for lawful purposes and in accordance with the license conditions. They may not copy, modify, distribute, reverse engineer, or resell the software without written permission from the licensor. These restrictions help prevent software piracy and protect proprietary technologies developed by the software company.

Payment terms are also included if the software requires a purchase or subscription. The agreement explains the licensing fees, subscription renewal schedule, refund policies, and any applicable taxes. Some agreements provide free software updates during the subscription period, while others require additional payments for major software upgrades. The document also specifies the consequences of failing to make timely payments, which may include suspension or termination of the license.

Many Software License Agreements contain warranty disclaimers and limitations of liability. While the software provider may guarantee that the software will perform substantially as described, they often disclaim responsibility for indirect damages, data loss, or business interruptions resulting from software failures. Users are encouraged to regularly back up their data and ensure that their computer systems meet the minimum hardware and software requirements.

The agreement also includes provisions regarding technical support, software updates, and termination of the license. Technical support services may be available for a specified period or as part of a maintenance subscription. The licensor may terminate the agreement if the licensee violates the terms of the contract, such as unauthorized copying or illegal distribution of the software. Upon termination, the licensee must stop using the software and remove all installed copies from their devices.

Finally, the agreement specifies the governing law, jurisdiction, and dispute resolution procedures. If disagreements arise regarding the interpretation or enforcement of the agreement, the parties may attempt negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or legal proceedings in the appropriate court. These provisions provide a structured process for resolving disputes while protecting the legal rights of both parties.

Overall, a Software License Agreement establishes clear rules for the lawful use of software products. By defining licensing rights, usage restrictions, payment obligations, warranty limitations, technical support, termination conditions, and dispute resolution procedures, the agreement promotes responsible software use while safeguarding the intellectual property and commercial interests of software developers and providers.
