# Legal Document 96 – AI Ethics and Governance Agreement

### Original Text

An AI Ethics and Governance Agreement is a legally binding contract between an organization and an AI governance service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for implementing ethical principles, governance frameworks, compliance measures, and oversight mechanisms for artificial intelligence systems. The agreement defines the scope of AI governance services, responsibilities of both parties, ethical standards, risk management procedures, regulatory compliance requirements, confidentiality obligations, intellectual property rights, payment terms, audit mechanisms, reporting requirements, and dispute resolution procedures. AI Ethics and Governance Agreements are commonly used by businesses, healthcare organizations, financial institutions, educational institutions, government agencies, research organizations, and technology companies to ensure that artificial intelligence systems are developed, deployed, and operated responsibly while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both parties. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that promotes accountability, transparency, fairness, and responsible AI governance.

The agreement begins by identifying the organization and the AI governance service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of governance services, and a detailed description of the artificial intelligence systems, machine learning models, automated decision-making tools, natural language processing systems, computer vision applications, recommendation engines, predictive analytics solutions, and intelligent automation platforms covered under the agreement. The agreement also identifies governance objectives, ethical principles, compliance requirements, reporting obligations, review schedules, and oversight responsibilities. Clearly defining the governance scope helps establish accountability and promotes consistent implementation of responsible AI practices.

One of the most important sections of the AI Ethics and Governance Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the governance service provider. The provider agrees to assess AI systems for ethical risks, establish governance policies, conduct fairness evaluations, review model transparency, monitor regulatory compliance, perform algorithmic impact assessments, document governance activities, provide compliance recommendations, and support the organization during regulatory reviews. The provider is responsible for identifying governance gaps, recommending corrective actions, maintaining governance records, and assisting with continuous improvement of AI oversight practices.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the organization. The organization agrees to provide accurate information regarding AI systems, supply technical documentation, cooperate during governance reviews, implement approved governance recommendations where appropriate, participate in compliance assessments, review governance reports, and make payments according to the agreed financial schedule. The organization is responsible for ensuring lawful operation of AI systems, maintaining internal governance structures, and complying with applicable laws governing artificial intelligence, privacy, cybersecurity, and consumer protection. Effective collaboration between both parties supports responsible AI governance.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies governance service fees, consulting charges, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and charges for additional services beyond the agreed scope. Additional services may include specialized compliance audits, bias testing, explainability assessments, governance training, regulatory advisory services, policy development, or expanded monitoring activities. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports transparent service management.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the AI governance engagement. Confidential information may include AI models, software source code, governance reports, technical documentation, customer records, employee information, financial reports, authentication credentials, business strategies, research information, proprietary technologies, and intellectual property. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes governance monitoring and regulatory compliance procedures. Both parties agree to conduct periodic governance reviews, evaluate compliance with applicable laws and ethical principles, assess fairness, transparency, accountability, privacy protection, cybersecurity, and human oversight mechanisms, document identified risks, implement corrective actions, and maintain governance records. The governance service provider agrees to support regulatory inspections, prepare governance reports, and recommend updates whenever legal or technological changes affect AI operations.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the AI Ethics and Governance Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet governance obligations, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, regulatory non-compliance, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the governance service provider agrees to return governance documentation, confidential information, compliance reports, and related project materials, securely remove retained client information where legally permitted, and provide reasonable transition assistance if requested.

Finally, the AI Ethics and Governance Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, audit rights, amendment procedures, governance reporting, periodic compliance reviews, regulatory updates, and continuous improvement initiatives. If disputes arise concerning governance services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, regulatory compliance, ethical AI practices, intellectual property ownership, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining governance responsibilities, ethical standards, financial obligations, confidentiality protections, compliance requirements, oversight procedures, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, an AI Ethics and Governance Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports responsible, transparent, and accountable governance of artificial intelligence systems while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the organization and the AI governance service provider.
