# Legal Document 100 – AI Model Evaluation and Validation Agreement

### Original Text

An AI Model Evaluation and Validation Agreement is a legally binding contract between a client and an AI evaluation service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for testing, validating, benchmarking, monitoring, and assessing the performance, reliability, fairness, security, and compliance of artificial intelligence models. The agreement defines the scope of evaluation services, responsibilities of both parties, testing methodologies, performance metrics, confidentiality obligations, intellectual property rights, payment terms, regulatory compliance requirements, reporting obligations, and dispute resolution mechanisms. AI Model Evaluation and Validation Agreements are commonly used by businesses, healthcare organizations, financial institutions, educational institutions, government agencies, research organizations, and technology companies to ensure that AI models operate accurately, securely, and responsibly while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both parties. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that supports trustworthy AI deployment and continuous performance improvement.

The agreement begins by identifying the client and the AI evaluation service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the evaluation engagement, and a detailed description of the AI models covered under the agreement. These models may include machine learning classifiers, regression models, deep learning systems, natural language processing applications, computer vision models, recommendation engines, fraud detection systems, predictive analytics solutions, or intelligent automation platforms. The agreement identifies evaluation objectives, datasets to be used, testing environments, validation methodologies, performance benchmarks, fairness criteria, security assessments, and reporting schedules. Clearly defining the evaluation scope helps establish expectations and ensures comprehensive assessment of AI model performance.

One of the most important sections of the AI Model Evaluation and Validation Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the evaluation service provider. The provider agrees to conduct functional testing, performance benchmarking, accuracy validation, robustness assessments, bias detection, explainability evaluations, security testing, compliance reviews, and documentation of evaluation results. The provider is responsible for preparing detailed evaluation reports, identifying model limitations, recommending corrective actions, maintaining testing records, protecting confidential information, and delivering services according to recognized AI evaluation standards and agreed project timelines.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to provide access to AI models, authorized evaluation datasets, technical documentation, operational requirements, testing environments, timely feedback, and necessary approvals required for successful evaluation activities. The client is responsible for ensuring that evaluation datasets have been lawfully obtained, reviewing evaluation reports, implementing approved recommendations where appropriate, participating in validation reviews, and making payments according to the agreed financial schedule. Effective collaboration between both parties contributes to accurate and reliable model assessment.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies evaluation service fees, milestone-based payments where applicable, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and charges for additional services beyond the agreed scope. Additional services may include repeated testing, adversarial security assessments, regulatory compliance audits, fairness monitoring, model retraining recommendations, performance optimization consulting, or post-deployment validation support. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports transparent project management.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the evaluation engagement. Confidential information may include AI models, software source code, datasets, evaluation reports, customer records, employee information, financial reports, authentication credentials, technical documentation, research information, business strategies, proprietary technologies, and intellectual property. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes regulatory compliance and quality assurance requirements. Both parties agree to comply with applicable laws governing artificial intelligence, privacy, cybersecurity, intellectual property, consumer protection, and data governance throughout the evaluation lifecycle. The evaluation service provider agrees to apply recognized testing methodologies, maintain objective assessment procedures, document all evaluation activities, and support regulatory reviews where required. Both parties also agree to periodically review evaluation processes and implement continuous improvements to enhance AI reliability, transparency, fairness, and accountability.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the AI Model Evaluation and Validation Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to perform agreed evaluation services, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, unlawful use of evaluation datasets, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the evaluation service provider agrees to deliver completed evaluation reports, return confidential information, securely delete retained client datasets where legally permitted, transfer agreed documentation, and provide reasonable transition assistance according to the agreement.

Finally, the AI Model Evaluation and Validation Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, audit rights, amendment procedures, project reporting, periodic quality reviews, regulatory updates, and continuous improvement initiatives. If disputes arise concerning evaluation services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property ownership, model performance assessments, regulatory compliance, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining evaluation responsibilities, testing procedures, financial obligations, confidentiality protections, compliance requirements, quality assurance standards, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, an AI Model Evaluation and Validation Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports reliable, secure, fair, and responsible evaluation of artificial intelligence systems while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the AI evaluation service provider.
