# Legal Document 94 – AI Model Deployment and Maintenance Agreement

### Original Text

An AI Model Deployment and Maintenance Agreement is a legally binding contract between a client and an AI deployment service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for deploying, monitoring, maintaining, updating, and supporting artificial intelligence models in production environments. The agreement defines the scope of deployment services, responsibilities of both parties, infrastructure requirements, model performance expectations, security requirements, confidentiality obligations, payment terms, compliance requirements, intellectual property rights, service level commitments, and dispute resolution mechanisms. AI Model Deployment and Maintenance Agreements are commonly used by businesses, healthcare organizations, financial institutions, educational institutions, government agencies, manufacturing companies, and technology enterprises to ensure that AI systems operate reliably, securely, and efficiently after deployment. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that supports long-term AI operations while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both parties.

The agreement begins by identifying the client and the AI deployment service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of deployment and maintenance services, and a detailed description of the AI models covered under the agreement. These models may include machine learning models, deep learning systems, natural language processing applications, computer vision models, recommendation engines, predictive analytics solutions, fraud detection systems, intelligent automation tools, or conversational AI applications. The agreement identifies deployment environments, infrastructure requirements, performance metrics, monitoring procedures, maintenance schedules, supported platforms, and expected service levels. Clearly defining the deployment scope helps establish service expectations and minimizes misunderstandings regarding operational responsibilities.

One of the most important sections of the AI Model Deployment and Maintenance Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the service provider. The provider agrees to deploy AI models into approved production environments, configure supporting infrastructure, monitor model performance, manage software updates, retrain models where authorized, resolve operational issues, optimize system performance, maintain technical documentation, and provide ongoing technical support according to agreed service levels. The provider is responsible for implementing appropriate security controls, monitoring system availability, maintaining operational logs, and addressing identified defects or performance degradation within agreed response times.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to provide access to approved deployment environments, maintain required infrastructure resources, supply updated datasets where applicable, review operational reports, approve scheduled maintenance activities, participate in acceptance testing, and provide timely feedback regarding system performance. The client is responsible for ensuring lawful use of operational datasets, maintaining compliance with applicable data protection laws, protecting user access credentials, and making payments according to the agreed financial schedule. Effective collaboration between both parties contributes to reliable AI system operations.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies recurring maintenance fees, technical support charges, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and charges for additional services beyond the agreed scope. Additional services may include emergency incident response, model retraining, cloud infrastructure optimization, integration with third-party platforms, performance tuning, feature enhancements, user training, or expanded monitoring services. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports transparent service management.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the AI deployment engagement. Confidential information may include trained AI models, software source code, datasets, customer records, employee information, financial reports, authentication credentials, encryption keys, technical documentation, research information, business strategies, proprietary technologies, and intellectual property. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes intellectual property ownership and operational governance requirements. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the client retains ownership of project-specific AI models, client-provided datasets, operational reports, and project deliverables upon full payment. The service provider retains ownership of pre-existing deployment frameworks, reusable automation scripts, proprietary monitoring tools, and independently developed technologies unless specifically transferred under the agreement. Both parties agree to comply with applicable laws governing artificial intelligence, privacy, data protection, cybersecurity, intellectual property, fairness, transparency, accountability, and responsible AI deployment throughout the operational lifecycle.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the AI Model Deployment and Maintenance Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet agreed service levels, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, unlawful use of datasets, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the service provider agrees to transfer deployment documentation, return confidential information, securely remove retained client datasets where legally permitted, provide completed operational reports, and offer reasonable transition assistance according to the agreement.

Finally, the AI Model Deployment and Maintenance Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, amendment procedures, service reviews, performance reporting, and change management. If disputes arise concerning AI deployment services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property ownership, dataset usage, model performance, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining deployment responsibilities, operational service standards, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, intellectual property protections, maintenance procedures, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, an AI Model Deployment and Maintenance Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports secure, reliable, and responsible deployment of artificial intelligence systems while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the AI deployment service provider.
