# Legal Document 30 – Service Level Agreement (SLA)

### Original Text

A Service Level Agreement (SLA) is a legally binding contract between a service provider and a customer that defines the level of service expected during the provision of a product or service. The agreement establishes measurable performance standards, responsibilities, service availability, response times, maintenance procedures, and remedies if the agreed service levels are not achieved. Service Level Agreements are widely used in information technology, cloud computing, telecommunications, managed services, software support, healthcare, banking, and outsourcing industries. By clearly defining service expectations, the agreement promotes accountability, improves customer satisfaction, and reduces the likelihood of disputes.

The agreement begins by identifying the service provider and the customer, including their legal names, business addresses, and contact information. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the service relationship, and a detailed description of the services covered under the SLA. This may include technical support, software maintenance, cloud hosting, network management, customer support, cybersecurity services, or infrastructure maintenance. Clearly describing the services helps ensure that both parties have a common understanding of the scope of the agreement.

One of the most important sections of the Service Level Agreement defines the performance metrics that the service provider must achieve. These metrics may include system availability, network uptime, response time for support requests, issue resolution time, backup frequency, disaster recovery objectives, and service reliability. For example, the agreement may require that a cloud service remain available for 99.9 percent of each calendar month, except during scheduled maintenance periods. Establishing measurable service standards enables both parties to objectively evaluate service performance.

The agreement also outlines the responsibilities of the service provider. The provider agrees to maintain qualified personnel, implement appropriate security measures, monitor system performance, perform preventive maintenance, and promptly respond to service interruptions. Regular maintenance schedules, software updates, security patches, and technical documentation may also be included to ensure the continued reliability of the service. The provider is expected to notify the customer in advance of planned maintenance activities that may temporarily affect service availability.

The customer also has responsibilities under the agreement. These may include providing accurate information, maintaining compatible hardware and software, reporting technical issues through approved communication channels, and cooperating with troubleshooting activities. The customer is expected to follow the provider's operational guidelines to ensure efficient delivery of services. Clearly defining customer responsibilities helps avoid delays in resolving technical issues.

The agreement specifies procedures for handling service failures and performance issues. If the provider fails to meet the agreed service levels, the customer may be entitled to service credits, refunds, additional support, or other remedies described in the agreement. The SLA also establishes procedures for reporting incidents, prioritizing support requests, escalating unresolved issues, and communicating progress until problems are fully resolved.

Confidentiality and data protection provisions require both parties to protect sensitive business information, customer records, technical documentation, system credentials, and other confidential materials exchanged during the service relationship. Both parties agree to comply with applicable data protection laws and implement reasonable security measures to prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of confidential information.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may end the agreement. Reasons for termination may include repeated failure to meet service levels, material breach of contract, non-payment, bankruptcy, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the service provider may be required to return customer data, assist with service migration, and securely delete confidential information in accordance with the agreement.

Finally, the Service Level Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, and legal compliance. If disagreements arise concerning service performance or contractual obligations, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining service expectations, performance standards, responsibilities, remedies, confidentiality obligations, and dispute resolution procedures, a Service Level Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that promotes reliable service delivery, strengthens business relationships, and protects the interests of both the service provider and the customer.
