# Legal Document 63 – Disaster Recovery Agreement

### Original Text

A Disaster Recovery Agreement is a legally binding contract between a client and a disaster recovery service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for preparing, implementing, and maintaining disaster recovery services to ensure business continuity following unexpected disruptions. The agreement defines the scope of recovery services, responsibilities of both parties, recovery objectives, security requirements, payment terms, confidentiality obligations, testing procedures, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Disaster Recovery Agreements are commonly used by businesses, financial institutions, healthcare organizations, educational institutions, government agencies, and technology companies to protect critical information systems, applications, databases, and business operations against natural disasters, cyberattacks, hardware failures, and other disruptive events. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that supports rapid recovery while protecting the legal and commercial interests of both parties.

The agreement begins by identifying the client and the disaster recovery service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the services, and a detailed description of the systems, applications, databases, cloud environments, and business processes covered under the recovery plan. The agreement also identifies critical business functions, backup infrastructure, alternate recovery locations, and communication procedures that will be used during disaster recovery operations. Clearly defining the recovery scope ensures that both parties understand the systems protected under the agreement.

One of the most important sections of the Disaster Recovery Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the service provider. The provider agrees to develop and maintain disaster recovery plans, perform regular data backups, monitor backup integrity, maintain redundant infrastructure, conduct disaster recovery testing, and restore critical systems according to agreed recovery objectives. The provider is responsible for responding promptly to disaster events, coordinating recovery activities, maintaining communication with the client, and documenting all recovery procedures performed during an incident. Regular testing helps verify that recovery plans remain effective and operational.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to provide accurate information regarding critical business systems, maintain updated inventories of hardware and software assets, notify the provider of significant infrastructure changes, participate in disaster recovery testing, and cooperate during recovery operations. The client is responsible for identifying business priorities, approving recovery strategies, maintaining internal emergency response procedures, and ensuring that employees are familiar with disaster recovery policies and communication protocols.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies recurring service fees, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and charges for additional recovery services beyond the agreed scope. Additional charges may apply for emergency recovery operations, infrastructure expansion, extended recovery support, cloud resource utilization, or specialized consulting services. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports long-term business relationships.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during disaster recovery planning and operations. Confidential information may include business continuity plans, system configurations, backup data, customer records, financial information, technical documentation, software source code, network architecture, encryption keys, and proprietary business information. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective even after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes recovery objectives and testing requirements. Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO) are defined to specify the maximum acceptable downtime and allowable data loss for critical business systems. The service provider agrees to conduct scheduled disaster recovery exercises, simulate emergency scenarios, evaluate recovery performance, identify weaknesses, and recommend improvements to enhance business resilience. Test results are documented and reviewed jointly by both parties to ensure continuous improvement of recovery capabilities.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Disaster Recovery Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet agreed recovery objectives, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the service provider agrees to return all client-owned backup media, recovery documentation, confidential information, and technical records, securely remove retained copies where legally permitted, and provide reasonable transition assistance if requested by the client.

Finally, the Disaster Recovery Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, and amendment procedures. If disputes arise concerning recovery services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, disaster response activities, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining disaster recovery responsibilities, recovery objectives, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, testing procedures, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Disaster Recovery Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports business continuity, minimizes operational disruption, and protects the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the disaster recovery service provider.
