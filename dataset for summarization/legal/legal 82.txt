# Legal Document 82 – Disaster Recovery Services Agreement

### Original Text

A Disaster Recovery Services Agreement is a legally binding contract between a client and a disaster recovery service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for planning, implementing, managing, and restoring information technology systems and business operations following disasters or major disruptions. The agreement defines the scope of disaster recovery services, responsibilities of both parties, recovery objectives, security requirements, confidentiality obligations, payment terms, compliance requirements, testing procedures, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Disaster Recovery Services Agreements are commonly used by businesses, financial institutions, healthcare organizations, educational institutions, government agencies, and technology companies to ensure business continuity in the event of cyberattacks, hardware failures, natural disasters, power outages, or other unexpected incidents. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that supports timely recovery of critical operations while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both parties.

The agreement begins by identifying the client and the disaster recovery service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the services, and a detailed description of the information systems, applications, databases, cloud environments, communication networks, and digital assets covered under the agreement. The agreement also identifies recovery time objectives (RTO), recovery point objectives (RPO), backup locations, disaster recovery sites, and categories of critical business functions requiring priority restoration. Clearly defining the recovery scope helps establish expectations and supports effective disaster preparedness.

One of the most important sections of the Disaster Recovery Services Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the service provider. The provider agrees to develop and maintain disaster recovery plans, establish secure recovery infrastructure, perform regular system backups, monitor disaster recovery readiness, conduct recovery testing, restore affected systems following authorized disaster declarations, and provide technical support throughout the recovery process. The provider is responsible for documenting recovery activities, maintaining recovery records, ensuring the integrity of restored systems, and continuously improving recovery procedures based on testing results and operational experience.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to identify critical business systems, provide accurate technical documentation, maintain updated inventories of information assets, cooperate during disaster recovery testing, review recovery plans, authorize disaster recovery activation when necessary, and participate in post-recovery evaluations. The client is responsible for ensuring employees understand disaster recovery procedures and comply with organizational business continuity policies. Effective collaboration between both parties contributes to efficient recovery operations.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies recurring service fees, emergency recovery charges, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and additional charges for extended recovery operations, dedicated recovery infrastructure, emergency technical support, or expanded disaster recovery services beyond the agreed scope. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports transparent service management.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the disaster recovery relationship. Confidential information may include customer records, employee information, financial reports, healthcare records, software source code, technical documentation, authentication credentials, encryption keys, recovery procedures, business continuity plans, research information, and proprietary technologies. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes disaster recovery testing and business continuity procedures. The service provider agrees to conduct periodic disaster recovery exercises, simulate recovery scenarios, verify backup integrity, test failover systems, evaluate recovery performance against agreed objectives, and provide detailed testing reports to the client. Both parties agree to review test results, identify areas for improvement, implement corrective actions, and update disaster recovery plans whenever significant changes occur in the technology environment or business operations.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Disaster Recovery Services Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet agreed recovery objectives, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the provider agrees to return client-owned recovery documentation, securely destroy retained backup data where legally permitted, discontinue recovery services, and provide reasonable transition assistance to support migration to another recovery solution if requested.

Finally, the Disaster Recovery Services Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, amendment procedures, and periodic service reviews. If disputes arise concerning disaster recovery services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, recovery procedures, business continuity planning, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining recovery responsibilities, recovery objectives, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, disaster recovery testing procedures, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Disaster Recovery Services Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports resilient business continuity and rapid restoration of critical information systems while protecting the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the disaster recovery service provider.
