# Legal Document 81 – Data Backup and Recovery Agreement

### Original Text

A Data Backup and Recovery Agreement is a legally binding contract between a client and a backup service provider that establishes the terms and conditions for backing up, storing, protecting, and recovering digital data in the event of accidental loss, system failure, cyberattacks, natural disasters, or other unforeseen incidents. The agreement defines the scope of backup services, responsibilities of both parties, backup schedules, recovery procedures, security requirements, confidentiality obligations, payment terms, compliance requirements, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Data Backup and Recovery Agreements are commonly used by businesses, financial institutions, healthcare organizations, educational institutions, government agencies, and technology companies to ensure business continuity and minimize the risks associated with data loss. The agreement provides a structured legal framework that supports secure data protection and reliable recovery while safeguarding the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both parties.

The agreement begins by identifying the client and the backup service provider, including their legal names, registered business addresses, and authorized representatives. It specifies the effective date of the agreement, the duration of the backup services, and a detailed description of the systems, databases, applications, cloud environments, file servers, virtual machines, and digital assets covered under the agreement. The agreement also identifies the backup frequency, retention periods, storage locations, recovery objectives, and categories of information included in the backup process. Clearly defining the scope of backup services helps establish common expectations and ensures comprehensive protection of organizational data.

One of the most important sections of the Data Backup and Recovery Agreement outlines the responsibilities of the backup service provider. The provider agrees to perform scheduled backups according to the agreed timetable, securely store backup copies, verify backup integrity, monitor backup operations, implement encryption for data in transit and at rest, maintain disaster recovery infrastructure, and restore client data promptly when authorized recovery requests are received. The provider is responsible for documenting backup activities, maintaining recovery logs, conducting periodic recovery testing, and ensuring that backup systems remain operational throughout the agreement period.

The agreement also specifies the responsibilities of the client. The client agrees to identify the data requiring backup, provide authorized access to covered systems, maintain accurate system documentation, notify the provider of significant infrastructure changes, review backup reports, and promptly report any issues affecting backup operations. The client is responsible for verifying the completeness of recovered data, maintaining compliance with applicable legal requirements, and ensuring that employees follow organizational data protection policies. Cooperation between both parties contributes to effective backup and recovery operations.

Financial provisions clearly define the commercial terms of the agreement. The document specifies recurring subscription fees, storage charges, billing schedules, payment methods, applicable taxes, reimbursement of approved expenses, and additional charges for expanded storage capacity, emergency recovery services, extended retention periods, or specialized disaster recovery assistance beyond the agreed service scope. Clearly documenting financial obligations helps reduce payment disputes and supports transparent service delivery.

Confidentiality provisions require both parties to protect confidential information exchanged during the backup and recovery relationship. Confidential information may include customer records, employee information, financial reports, healthcare records, software source code, technical documentation, authentication credentials, encryption keys, security configurations, business strategies, research data, and proprietary technologies. Neither party may disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals without prior written consent unless disclosure is required by law. These confidentiality obligations generally remain effective after the agreement has expired or been terminated.

The agreement also establishes disaster recovery and business continuity procedures. If data loss, hardware failure, ransomware attacks, natural disasters, or other disruptions occur, the backup service provider agrees to initiate recovery procedures according to the agreed recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO). Both parties agree to cooperate during recovery operations, verify restored data, investigate the cause of the incident, and implement corrective measures to reduce the likelihood of future disruptions.

Termination provisions explain the circumstances under which either party may terminate the Data Backup and Recovery Agreement. Reasons for termination may include material breach of contract, repeated failure to meet agreed service levels, non-payment, insolvency, violation of confidentiality obligations, or mutual written agreement. Upon termination, the provider agrees to return client-owned backup data where requested, securely destroy retained backup copies where legally permitted, discontinue backup services, and provide documentation confirming data disposal or transfer according to the agreement.

Finally, the Data Backup and Recovery Agreement includes provisions regarding governing law, dispute resolution, limitation of liability, legal compliance, audit rights, amendment procedures, and periodic service reviews. If disputes arise concerning backup services, payment obligations, confidentiality requirements, recovery procedures, service performance, or interpretation of the agreement, the parties agree to first attempt negotiation or mediation before pursuing arbitration or court proceedings. By clearly defining backup responsibilities, recovery procedures, financial obligations, confidentiality requirements, disaster recovery measures, termination conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms, a Data Backup and Recovery Agreement provides a comprehensive legal framework that supports secure, reliable, and efficient protection of organizational data while safeguarding the legal, technical, and commercial interests of both the client and the backup service provider.
